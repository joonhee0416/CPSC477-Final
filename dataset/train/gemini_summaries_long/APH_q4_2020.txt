Amphenol reported record fourth-quarter 2020 revenue of $2.426 billion, with organic growth of 11%. Sales were driven by mobile devices, industrial, and automotive end markets, with adjusted earnings per share increasing by 15% year-over-year to $1.13 and diluted GAAP earnings per share reaching $1.15. Despite pandemic-related challenges, the Company's full-year adjusted operating margin remained at 19.2%, with adjusted diluted earnings per share remaining unchanged at $3.74 compared to 2019, reflecting the strength of the Company's performance and cost management. Amphenol continues to expand its product offerings through acquisitions, including MTS Systems, Positronic, and El-Cab, and has announced a 2-for-1 stock split. For the first quarter of 2021, the Company expects sales growth of 14-17% and adjusted diluted earnings per share growth of 27-32% compared to the same period in 2020.
