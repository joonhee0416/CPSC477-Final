In Q4 2021, Marathon Petroleum Corporation (MPC) reported strong financial results, with adjusted EBITDA increasing by $400 million from the previous quarter. The Refining & Marketing segment performed well, driven by higher refining margins and increased throughput, particularly in the U.S. Gulf Coast and West Coast regions. MPC remains focused on cost reduction, with $1.5 billion in cost reductions since 2020. This includes $1 billion in refining cost reductions and $400 million in midstream cost reductions. The company is also committed to shareholder returns, and since November 2021, it has repurchased $3 billion of shares, representing 55% completion of its $10 billion share repurchase program. Additionally, MPC announced an incremental $5 billion share repurchase authorization, bringing the total authorization to $15 billion. The company's balance sheet remains strong, with $10.8 billion in cash and short-term investments and an investment-grade credit profile. For 2022, MPC projects total throughput volumes of 2.9 million barrels per day and capital spending of $1.7 billion, with approximately 50% allocated to the Martinez refinery conversion to a renewable fuels facility.
