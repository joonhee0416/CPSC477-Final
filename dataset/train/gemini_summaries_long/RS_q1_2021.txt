Reliance Steel & Aluminum's first quarter performance was record-setting, marked by a 20% increase in average selling prices, a 33% jump in sales over the fourth quarter of 2020, and a 11.3% increase in tons sold compared to the fourth quarter. The company's resilient business model and strong execution in a favorable market resulted in record gross profit margins, contributing to record pre-tax income of $359 million, up 100% from the prior quarter. The demand in nonresidential construction is improving, and despite disruptions, the demand for toll processing services in the automotive market remains strong. Reliance anticipates strong demand continuing in the noncommercial aerospace market for the rest of the year. The company's gross profit margin in the second quarter of 2021 is expected to remain strong, with average selling prices estimated to be up 5% to 7%. Reliance anticipates non-GAAP earnings per diluted share in the range of $4.20 and $4.40 in the second quarter of 2021. The company remains optimistic about business conditions and is investing in its growth and returning value to stockholders.
