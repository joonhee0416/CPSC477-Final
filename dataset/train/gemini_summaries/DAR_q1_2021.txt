Darling Ingredients reported a record combined EBITDA of $284.8 million in Q1 2021, driven by higher commodity prices. The Feed segment's EBITDA surged to $124.4 million, a significant increase from the same period last year. The Food segment also performed well, with an EBITDA of $46.4 million, primarily due to solid growth in collagen peptide sales. Additionally, the Fuel segment reported $128.7 million of combined EBITDA, with Diamond Green Diesel generating a robust $108.2 million EBITDA. Net income rose to $151.8 million and net sales increased by 22.7% to $1.05 billion. The company raised its guidance for the Fuel segment due to DGD's exceptional performance, projecting DGD's EBITDA to range between $410 million and $435 million for 2021. Darling continues to invest in innovation, such as sustainable insect ingredients through EnviroFlight and biomedical technologies through its X-Pure GelMA product line.
