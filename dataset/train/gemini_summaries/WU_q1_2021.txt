In Q1 2021, Western Union's revenue increased 2% to $1.2 billion. The Cross-Border Consumer-to-Consumer (C2C) business grew substantially, with a 28% increase in principal and a 15% increase in principal per transaction. Digital revenue, including Wu.com and digital partnerships, surged 45%, driven by strong user growth and expansion. Despite challenges from COVID-19, the company maintained stability in April, giving confidence in continued growth. Full-year 2021 guidance remains positive, with an expected mid to high single-digit revenue growth on a GAAP basis. Operating margin is forecasted to be approximately 21.5%, while the effective tax rate is projected to be in the mid-teens range. Adjusted earnings per share are estimated to be between $2.00 and $2.10. Western Union remains optimistic about the long-term prospects of serving the global migrant community, highlighting the market's size, growth potential, and spending power.
