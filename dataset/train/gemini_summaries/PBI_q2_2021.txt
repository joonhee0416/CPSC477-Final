During the second quarter of 2021, Pitney Bowes reported revenue growth of 6% at constant currency, with EBIT growth of 16%. Notably, SendTech's revenue increase was driven by its SendPro product family and double-digit growth in SaaS-based shipping. Presort also experienced recovery in volumes and improved EBIT margins, while Global e-commerce achieved positive EBITDA and improved EBIT. The company reported $0.11 in adjusted earnings per share, including a $0.03 tax benefit. Looking ahead, Pitney Bowes expects low to mid-single-digit annual revenue growth at constant currency and adjusted earnings per share in the $0.35 to $0.42 range. Despite challenges in the macro environment and supply chain, the company remains committed to its financial objectives and is confident in its long-term growth prospects.
