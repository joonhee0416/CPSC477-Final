Leggett & Platt reported strong first-quarter results with a 10% increase in sales to $1.15 billion, driven by volume growth, price increases, and currency benefits. EBIT rose to a record $128 million, leading to a significant increase in EBIT and EBITDA margins. Notably, earnings per share hit a first-quarter record of $0.64, reflecting a 60% increase compared to the adjusted EPS in Q1 2020. The company also announced a 5% increase in its quarterly dividend to $0.42 per share, marking its 50th consecutive year of dividend increases. Management increased its 2021 sales guidance to $4.8-$5.0 billion and EPS to $2.55-$2.75 due to higher raw material prices and improved volume growth. To align with inventory valuation practices of its peers, the company transitioned from LIFO to FIFO accounting, resulting in $21 million in expected tax payments over the next three years.
