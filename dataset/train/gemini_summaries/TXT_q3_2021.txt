Textron reported solid third-quarter results, reflecting revenue growth to $3 billion. Adjusted income from continuing operations rose to $0.85 per share, with strong performance across the company. Aviation saw a recovery in the general aviation market, with increased Citation jet deliveries, pushing backlog to $3.5 billion. Bell saw a dip in revenues due to lower military sales, but remained optimistic about its FARA program. Despite supply chain challenges, Industrial revenues remained strong due to higher pricing. Overall, Textron generated $271 million in manufacturing cash flow in the quarter, prompting an increase in its full-year manufacturing cash flow forecast to $1 billion-$1.1 billion. The company also raised its adjusted earnings per share guidance to $3.20-$3.30 for the year.
