Sunoco reported strong Q2 2021 financial results, showcasing its business model's strength. Revenue rose 28% year-over-year to 1.93 billion gallons. Despite rising commodity prices, adjusted EBITDA increased to $201 million, with a fuel margin of $0.113 per gallon and a coverage ratio of 1.67 times. Distributable cash flow as adjusted totaled $145 million. The company declared an $0.8255 per unit distribution. Operating expenses remained stable at $102 million and are expected to trend towards the low end of the guidance range. Sunoco's full-year adjusted EBITDA guidance remains unchanged at $725-$765 million, with maintenance capital expenditures of approximately $45 million and target growth capital expenditures of $150 million. To diversify and strengthen its business, Sunoco recently acquired eight refined product terminals with 14.8 million barrels of storage for a sub-seven times multiple on expected EBITDA and a gasoline and distillate terminal for a sub-six times multiple.
