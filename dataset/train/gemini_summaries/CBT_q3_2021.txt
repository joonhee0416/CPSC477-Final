Cabot had a strong third quarter, with adjusted earnings per share of $1.35, marking the second-best quarterly performance in the company's history. Demand was robust across all businesses, and the team successfully managed challenges like COVID-19 and supply chain disruptions. The company continued progress in targeted growth initiatives, particularly in battery applications. Cabot has commercial sales to six of the top eight EV battery producers globally, leveraging its product line breadth, technical expertise, and global footprint. The company remains optimistic about the fourth quarter despite expected lower volumes and insurance proceeds. Cabot has raised its full-year adjusted earnings per share outlook to $4.85 to $5.05, fueled by strong execution, smart investments, and a focus on key account management, data analytics, and OEE optimizations. The company's growth investments are well-positioned to capitalize on trends in mobility, alternative energy, and infrastructure reinvestment.
