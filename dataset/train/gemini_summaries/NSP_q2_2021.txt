In Q2 2021, the company achieved adjusted earnings per share of $0.91 and $60 million in adjusted EBITDA, with worksite employee growth exceeding the forecasted range at 7% and client retention remaining high at 99%. Despite a decline in gross profit due to increased healthcare utilization and lower unemployment tax rates, the company experienced favorable results in its workers' compensation program. Operating expenses increased due to investments in marketing and sales, while financial liquidity remained strong with stock buybacks and dividend increases. Management highlighted the growing importance of HR support, particularly in areas such as diversity and employee retention. The company raised its full-year guidance, projecting worksite employee growth of 5.5% to 6.5% and adjusted EPS of $4.00 to $4.59. Q3 guidance includes adjusted EBITDA of $52 million to $62 million and adjusted EPS of $0.74 to $0.93.
