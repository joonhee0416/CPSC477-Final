National Retail Properties reports a strong start to 2021, announcing an approximately 6% increase in core FFO guidance to a range of $2.70 to $2.75 per share. The company's portfolio of 3,161 retail properties maintained a high occupancy rate of 98.3% in Q1, with impressive rent collections of 97% and 98% for Q1 and April, respectively. Despite the pandemic, the firm's occupancy, leasing, and rent collection outcomes remain robust. National Retail Properties also acquired 29 new properties at an initial cash cap rate of 6.4% and sold 11 properties to raise capital for acquisitions. The company's financial position is strong, with no debt maturities until 2024, an average debt duration of over 13 years, and a $311 million cash balance. The company's Q1 core FFO per share of $0.76 includes nonrecurring items such as receivables from cash basis tenants and lease termination fees.
