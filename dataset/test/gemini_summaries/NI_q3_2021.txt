NiSource exceeded expectations in Q3 2021, achieving $0.11 non-GAAP diluted NOEPS compared to $0.09 in Q3 2020. The company revised its 2021 guidance to target the upper range of $1.32 to $1.36 NOEPS and initiated guidance for 2022 within the 5% to 7% growth target at $1.42 to $1.48. NiSource's long-term growth target of 7% to 9% diluted NOEPS through 2024 remains, with the company's investments in renewable projects and infrastructure driving growth. Notably, NIPSCO plans to retire coal-fired generation between 2026 and 2028, investing up to $750 million in clean energy sources. Regulatory advancements include a settlement in Pennsylvania, proposed order in Maryland, and filed rate cases in Kentucky and Indiana. NiSource remains financially sound with $1.7 billion in net available liquidity and reaffirmed investment-grade credit ratings.
