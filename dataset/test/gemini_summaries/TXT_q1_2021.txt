Textron's Q1 earnings per share were $0.75, with adjusted EPS at $0.70, up from $0.35 in Q1 2020. This was driven by a 330 basis point increase in segment margins, with strong results across all segments. Bell saw increased commercial revenues, delivering 17 helicopters and securing a $293 million contract for the second phase of FLRAA. Textron Systems benefited from a $607 million contract for shadow system sustainment and modernization. Aviation delivered 28 jets and 14 turboprops, growing its backlog by $450 million to $2.1 billion. Industrial revenues increased due to higher volume and pricing in specialized vehicles, while Kautex saw doubled volumes of fuel systems for hybrid electric vehicles. Cash performance was strong, with manufacturing cash flow before pension contributions totaling $71 million, a $501 million improvement. Adjusted EPS guidance for the full year has been raised by $0.10 to a range of $2.80 to $3 per share.
