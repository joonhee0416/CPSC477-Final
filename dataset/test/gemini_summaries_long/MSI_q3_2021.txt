Despite supply chain challenges, the company reported a strong Q3 with a 13% revenue growth to $2.1 billion, driven by increased demand across all technologies. Revenue grew 11% in LMR, 26% in video security, and 13% in command center software. Operating margins expanded by 150 basis points, resulting in a record Q3 ending backlog of $11.4 billion. Based on this performance, the company raised its full-year guidance to revenue growth of 10% to 10.25% and non-GAAP earnings per share of $9.00 to $9.04. The strong backlog, ongoing customer investment in services, and expansion of relationships within the installed base provide visibility for continued growth in 2022, even amid ongoing supply chain challenges.
