ITW reported notable financial results for Q4 and full-year 2021, driven by strong execution and the "Win the Recovery" strategy. Organic growth reached 12% for the year, with each segment contributing between 6% and 18%. Despite supply chain challenges, Automotive OEM remains a key driver for long-term growth. Food Equipment led Q4 with 21% organic growth, while Test and Measurement and Electronics saw 11% growth, supported by semiconductor demand. Other notable segments include Welding (15%) and Construction (12%). GAAP earnings per share for Q4 was $1.93, with full-year earnings reaching an all-time high at $8.51, an increase of 28%. ITW's strong momentum is expected to continue in 2022, with guidance projecting organic growth of 6% to 9% and earnings growth of 10% to 15%. The company also plans to return $1.5 billion to shareholders through share buybacks and remains committed to selective acquisitions and divestitures to enhance growth potential.
