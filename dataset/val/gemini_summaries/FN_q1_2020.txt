Revenue in the first quarter of fiscal year 2020 was $399.3 million, exceeding the high end of guidance and representing a 6% increase from the previous year. Non-GAAP net income per share was $0.86, also exceeding guidance despite foreign exchange and interest rate headwinds. Notably, telecom revenue within optical communications grew 7% from the previous quarter and is expected to maintain momentum in Q2. In contrast, datacom revenue declined by 15% but is expected to stabilize in Q2. Overall, the company is optimistic about its future with strong leadership in complex product contract manufacturing and the establishment of a new product introduction facility in Israel. Non-GAAP gross margin improved to 12% in Q1, and the company expects further incremental improvements within its target range of 12% to 12.5% for Q2. Q2 revenue is projected to be between $408 million and $416 million, with non-GAAP net income per share expected to be in the range of $0.91 to $0.94.
