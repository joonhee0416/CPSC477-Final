In Q3 2020, the company achieved $0.91 adjusted earnings per share, a 21% increase over Q3 2019, and adjusted EBITDA of $58 million, a 13% increase. These results exceeded expectations due to growth in worksite employees and pricing, as well as cost savings from the pandemic. The average number of paid worksite employees increased by 1.7% sequentially in Q3, pushing the company closer to pre-pandemic levels. Gross profit increased by 8% over Q3 2019, driven by higher-than-anticipated worksite employee volume and pricing. For Q4, the company forecasts sequential growth in worksite employees within a range of 236,500 to 238,500, resulting in an expected decrease of only 1% for the entire year of 2020. Additionally, the company raised its full-year 2020 earnings guidance to an adjusted EBITDA of $271 million to $281 million and adjusted EPS of $4.35 to $4.53.
