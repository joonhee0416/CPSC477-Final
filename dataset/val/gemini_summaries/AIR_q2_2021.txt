Despite the ongoing pandemic's impact on commercial travel, the company experienced stabilized volumes and robust government business. Revenue declined 28% year-over-year, dropping from $561 million to $404 million, while adjusted diluted earnings per share from continuing operations decreased 52% to $0.31. However, the company made progress in margin improvement, with Aviation Services leading the way with a 6% sequential growth and a surge in MRO business. Government and defense sales accounted for 52% of total sales in the quarter, driven by strong contract performances. The company has also secured significant new business with a total contract value of $1.7 billion, positioning itself for growth in the future. Additionally, the company has settled with the Department of Justice for $8 million and maintained a strong balance sheet with net leverage below 1 times EBITDA.
