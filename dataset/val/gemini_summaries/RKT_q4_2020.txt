Rocket Companies reported strong results for 2020, driven by continued investment in its platform, brand, and talent. The company's platform, which includes its technology, data science, and brand, saw significant enhancements with nearly 4,500 product features deployed throughout the year and improvements delivered every 28 minutes on average. Rocket Companies' strong balance sheet, with $2 billion in cash and $7.7 billion in total liquidity, allows it to invest in growth initiatives and return capital to shareholders. The company has declared a special dividend of $1.11 per Class A common share, funded by an equity distribution of $2.2 billion. Rocket Companies expects continued growth in the first quarter of 2021, with closed loan volume projected to be between $98 billion and $103 billion, net rate lock volume between $88 billion and $95 billion, and gain-on-sale margins between 3.6% and 3.9%. The company's platform and entrepreneurial approach continue to drive innovation and expansion into new business models, with Rocket Auto, Rocket Loans, and Rocket Homes showing significant growth potential.
