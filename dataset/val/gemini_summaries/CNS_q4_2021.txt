Cohen & Steers reported record earnings per share of $1.24 for Q4 2021, up from $0.76 in Q4 2020. Revenue reached a record $159.7 million, driven by higher average assets under management and performance fees. Operating income surged to $82.6 million, resulting in a record operating margin of 51.7% due to a cumulative adjustment that reduced compensation and benefits. Assets under management totaled $106.6 billion, marking a 10% increase from Q3 2021. The company experienced net inflows of $1.8 billion, with contributions from various segments including wirehouse, regional brokerage, and RIA. Institutional advisory channel had record net inflows of $1.9 billion in 2021, and the pipeline of awarded but unfunded mandates has increased significantly to $2.1 billion. The company plans to launch new initiatives and products, including a new closed-end fund, to capitalize on momentum and position itself for continued growth in real asset strategies.
