Despite pandemic challenges, Reliance Steel & Aluminum experienced a solid financial performance in 2020 due to strong operational execution and a resilient business model. The company achieved consecutive years of record gross profit margins at 31.5%, exceeding their sustainable range. Despite a decline in profitability, non-GAAP earnings per share reached $7.71. Reliance maintained healthy cash flow from operations and achieved an inventory turn goal of 4.7 times. Looking ahead to Q1 2021, the company anticipates a 10-12% increase in tons sold and a 12-14% rise in average selling price compared to Q4 2020. This is expected to result in non-GAAP earnings per share of $3.40 to $3.50 for Q1 2021. The company highlighted the importance of its safety program, which resulted in a 23% reduction in the incident rate compared to 2019. Reliance also announced leadership promotions within its finance department.
