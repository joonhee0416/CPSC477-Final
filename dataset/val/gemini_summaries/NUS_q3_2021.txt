Nu Skin's Q3 revenue reached $641.2 million, indicating a decline of 8.8% compared to Q3 2020. However, this represents an 8.7% growth over Q3 2019. The company reported earnings per share of $0.97, representing a 10% year-over-year decrease but a 22.8% increase compared to the same period in 2019. Nu Skin's key strategic imperatives for growth include launching personalized beauty and wellness strategies, evolving their go-to-market approach, and expanding their digital platform. They are optimistic about the future, as they aim to achieve growth by introducing next-generation connected devices and expanding their social commerce business model. For 2021, Nu Skin projects revenue between $2.67 to $2.70 billion, with earnings per share anticipated to range from $3.93 to $4.03.
