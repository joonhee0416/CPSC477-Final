Interpublic Group (IPG) reported strong financial results for the second quarter of 2021, driven by robust organic growth across its client sectors and disciplines. Net organic revenue increased by 19.8% year-over-year to $2.27 billion, with double-digit growth in all eight major client sectors, including healthcare, retail, and auto. IPG's IAN segment grew 20.5% organically, led by media, data, and technology, while its DXTRA segment experienced 15.1% organic growth, reflecting recovery in sports and entertainment offerings. Despite the ongoing pandemic, IPG maintained strong profitability with an adjusted EBITDA margin of 17.9%, driven by cost savings from restructuring and lower variable expenses. The company upgraded its full-year 2021 performance outlook, now expecting organic growth of 9%-10% and an adjusted EBITDA margin of approximately 16%. IPG remains focused on ESG initiatives, diversity, equity, and inclusion, and has taken steps to address media equity and climate change concerns. As the economy recovers and restrictions ease, IPG anticipates a gradual increase in travel and office-related expenses in the second half of the year, most notably in the fourth quarter.
