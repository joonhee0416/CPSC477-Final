Textron reported mixed fourth-quarter results, with revenues declining $368 million to $3.7 billion. Excluding one-time charges and benefits, adjusted earnings per share remained stable at $1.06. The company experienced margin improvements in Systems, Industrial, and Bell, leading to an overall manufacturing margin of 8.8% despite lower revenues. Bell's commercial helicopter deliveries declined, but the military V-22 program continued to grow. Textron Aviation delivered fewer jets and reported lower aftermarket volume, but new product launches are anticipated. For 2021, Textron projects revenue growth in all segments and adjusted earnings per share in the range of $2.70 to $2.90.
