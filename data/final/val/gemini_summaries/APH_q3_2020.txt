Amphenol, a global interconnect and sensor manufacturer, reported record sales and earnings per share in Q3 2020, exceeding guidance despite the challenges of the COVID-19 pandemic. Sales increased 11% to $2,323 million, driven by growth in mobile devices, IT-datacom, industrial, and automotive markets. Organic sales increased 9%. Operating income increased significantly, with margins expanding 80 basis points year-over-year, reflecting strong conversion and cost reduction efforts. The company generated $398 million in operating cash flow and repurchased 1.9 million shares, returning capital to shareholders. Full-year 2020 sales are expected to increase 1-2%, while adjusted diluted earnings per share are projected to decline 3-4% due to pandemic-related expenses. The company remains optimistic about its long-term prospects despite market uncertainties, citing its broad end-market diversification, strong financial position, and focus on next-generation interconnect and sensor technologies.
