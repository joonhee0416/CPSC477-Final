UniFirst Corporation reported a strong third quarter, with revenue increasing 4.2% year-over-year to $464.3 million, surpassing analyst estimates of $454.4 million. Diluted earnings per share rose significantly, from $1.12 to $2.21. The Core Laundry segment drove growth, contributing $409 million in revenue and benefiting from increased customer reopenings and PPE sales. Specialty Garments also performed well, exceeding expectations with revenue of $38.2 million. The company's First Aid segment experienced a decline in revenue but remained profitable. Management anticipates that costs, including labor and merchandise, will rise in fiscal 2022, potentially impacting margins. Despite these headwinds, UniFirst's solid balance sheet and continued investment in growth initiatives position it well to navigate the evolving economic landscape. The company projects fiscal 2021 revenue between $1.810 billion and $1.817 billion and diluted earnings per share between $7.80 and $8.00.
