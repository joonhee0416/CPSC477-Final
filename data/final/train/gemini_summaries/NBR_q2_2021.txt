Nabors reported strong second-quarter results, with adjusted EBITDA exceeding $117 million. The company's drilling operations performed well, with revenue increases across all segments except Canada. Daily rig margins in the Lower 48 remained robust at over $7,000, and international adjusted EBITDA grew by 14% sequentially. Nabors' technology solutions segment also saw growth, driven by strong performance in software and casing running services. The company generated free cash flow of $68 million and reduced its net debt by $58 million. Nabors highlighted key focus areas, including maintaining strong Lower 48 drilling margins, expanding its international business, investing in technology and innovation, deleveraging its balance sheet, and pursuing opportunities in the energy transition. The company expects continued increases in drilling activity and pricing in the second half of 2021 and remains committed to capital discipline and cash generation.
