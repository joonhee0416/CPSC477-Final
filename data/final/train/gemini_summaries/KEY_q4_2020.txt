Key reported strong Q4 2020 earnings with a record net income of $549 million ($0.56 per share). Despite the economic downturn, the company's dual mandate of pandemic response and strategic positioning for growth was successfully executed. Total average loans increased by 9% to $102 billion, driven by growth in commercial and consumer loans. Consumer mortgage originations reached an all-time high of $8.3 billion, while investment banking generated a record $243 million in fees. Net interest income rose by nearly 4%, while fee income climbed double-digits. Expenses were elevated due to production incentives, severance, and COVID-related expenses, but are expected to decrease in 2021. Credit quality remained strong, with net charge-offs in line with the targeted range. The company maintains a strong capital position with a Common Equity Tier 1 ratio of 9.8%, allowing for ongoing shareholder returns through buybacks and dividends.
