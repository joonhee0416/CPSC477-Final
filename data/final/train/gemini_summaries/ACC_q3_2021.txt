Student housing exceeded expectations post-pandemic, with strong occupancy and rental rate growth in Q3 2021. The sector has seen a surge in first-year student enrollment and total enrollment, with early leasing data indicating robust demand for the 2022 season. The industry is experiencing favorable tailwinds, including reduced new supply and increasing demand for on-campus modernization. American Campus Communities reported an opening fall occupancy of 95.8% with rental rate growth exceeding initial guidance. The company remains optimistic about its financial performance, raising its 2021 earnings guidance by 4%. The student housing industry's investment thesis remains intact, attracting institutional capital and prompting American Campus Communities to accelerate its disposition activity to strengthen its balance sheet. The company expects continued growth beyond 2022, projecting FFOM per share growth in the range of 12% to 15%.
