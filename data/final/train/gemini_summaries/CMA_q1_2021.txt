The company has generated strong earnings per share of $2.43 in the first quarter, primarily driven by improving credit quality. Strong credit performance and an improvement in the economic forecast resulted in a negative provision of $182 million. The economic metrics are improving quickly, and the outlook for the second half of the year is for strong economic growth. They have prudent management of deposit pricing, adding rate floors when possible as loans renew. The company also remains committed to maintaining strong expense discipline. Non-interest income has increased in each of the past four quarters, and the company has made technology investments to enhance the customer and colleague experience. The dividend yield is approximately 4%, and the company plans to restart share repurchases in the second quarter with a target of achieving 10% CET1.
