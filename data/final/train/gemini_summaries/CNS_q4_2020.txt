Cohen & Steers reported record quarterly earnings of $0.76 per share, up from $0.74 in the prior year and $0.67 sequentially. Revenue reached $116.6 million, surpassing the previous quarter's $111.4 million. Despite lower performance fees, the company experienced higher average assets under management. Operating income increased to $49.4 million, with an improved operating margin of 42.4%. Assets under management reached a record $79.9 billion, driven by net inflows of $3.9 billion and market appreciation. The company anticipates a decline in its compensation-to-revenue ratio to 35.5% in 2021 and expects G&A expenses to increase by 6% due to investments in technology, marketing, and travel expenses. The implied effective fee rate is projected to rise by approximately one basis point in 2021.
