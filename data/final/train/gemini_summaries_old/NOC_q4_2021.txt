Northrop Grumman reported solid 2021 results, executing its strategy for growth, performance, and value creation. Organic sales increased by 3%, segment operating margin expanded by 40 basis points to 11.8%, and transaction-adjusted earnings per share grew by 8%. Capital deployment included a record $4.7 billion returned to shareholders and a $3.1 billion transaction-adjusted free cash flow. Amidst COVID-19 challenges, the company strengthened its balance sheet, reducing debt by $2.2 billion and achieving a higher credit rating.

Looking ahead to 2022, Northrop Grumman expects continued organic growth driven by a robust backlog. Despite COVID-19 headwinds, they anticipate a modest sales increase and an 11.7% to 11.9% segment operating margin rate. Transaction-adjusted earnings per share are projected between $24.50 and $25.10, with double-digit free cash flow growth through 2024.

Notably, the company highlighted its portfolio alignment with customer priorities, including the NASA Artemis mission, Space Interceptor programs, and the F-35 program. They also emphasized their commitment to sustainability, ranking in the 96th percentile of S&P's Global Corporate Sustainability Assessment and working towards renewable energy goals.

Overall, Northrop Grumman delivered a positive earnings performance in 2021, remains well-positioned for continued growth, and is committed to shareholder returns through dividend payments and share repurchases.
