Old Republic International Corporation reported strong financial results for 2019, with flat net income and a 10.8% return on equity. The company's strategic diversification between its Title insurance and general insurance businesses contributed to steady earnings. The General Insurance group experienced mid-single-digit growth in net premiums earned and operating revenues, with a 97.5% composite ratio. Commercial auto claim ratios remain elevated due to systemic increases in claims severity, while workers' compensation claim ratios improved significantly. The Title Insurance Group set new records for underwriting revenues and profitability, with a 15.1% market share. Overall, Old Republic's balance sheet remains strong, with a composite ratio of 95.1%, a book value per share increase of 16%, and a return on book value of 26%. The company emphasized its commitment to selective business growth, sustainable profitability, and risk management.
