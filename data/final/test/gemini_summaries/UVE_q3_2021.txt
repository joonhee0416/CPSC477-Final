In Q3, the company achieved 15% growth in direct premiums earned, primarily due to primary rate increases in Florida. Expenses decreased, leading to a 16.4% annualized return on average equity. The combined ratio improved by 36.1 points, driven by decreased weather events and lower prior year reserve development. The expense ratio improved on both direct premiums earned and net bases. Net investment income and realized gains decreased due to reinvestment at lower yields. The company repurchased 101,000 shares and declared a quarterly cash dividend of $0.16 per share. Despite a 7.8% decrease in total revenue, adjusted EPS reached $0.63 on a non-GAAP basis. The company maintains its 2021 guidance of GAAP and non-GAAP adjusted EPS in the range of $2.75 to $3, with a return on average equity between 17% and 19%.
