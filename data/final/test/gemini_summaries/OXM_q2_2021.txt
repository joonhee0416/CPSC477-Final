Oxford Industries' earnings call highlighted strong performance in Q2, exceeding 2019 levels. Direct-to-consumer channels (full price, e-commerce, and retail stores) grew 22%, with e-commerce alone growing 49%. All brands reported double-digit growth, led by Lilly Pulitzer with 31%. Improved gross margins, expense control, and positive comparable sales contributed to operating margin improvement, with Tommy Bahama seeing a 1,000 basis point increase. The company remains focused on its long-term strategy of providing exceptional customer experiences and capitalizing on the trend towards casual, easy-care clothing. They are investing in technology, marketing, and physical locations to enhance their omnichannel presence. Adjusted earnings per share for the full year are projected at $6.45 to $6.70, a significant increase compared to $4.32 in 2019.
