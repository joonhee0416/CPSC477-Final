Despite the challenges of 2020, the firm performed admirably, with strong portfolio performance and an exceptional fourth quarter. Occupancy levels reached 95.7%, driven by leasing at developments and replacement tenants. Cash rental rates grew 13.5%, the second highest in company history. Encouraged by market dynamics, the firm plans to capitalize on land holdings and new acquisitions for growth. Approximately 54% of 2021 rollovers have been signed at a 13% cash rental rate increase, in line with the 10-14% increase expected for the full year. Notable leasing wins include the First Cypress Creek Commerce Center in South Florida, fully leased to a single e-commerce tenant, and leasing at multiple facilities in the Inland Empire. New development starts are planned in Phoenix, the Inland Empire, and Nashville, with total investment of approximately $99 million and targeted cash yields ranging from 6.3% to 7.2%. The firm successfully acquired its partners' interest in the PV 303 spec building in Phoenix and sold 15 properties during the quarter for $97.1 million. Based on strong performance and 2021 outlook, the Board of Directors declared a dividend of $0.27 per share for the first quarter of 2021, an 8% increase from 2020. The firm's balance sheet remains strong, with a net debt-to-adjusted EBITDA ratio of 4.8 times and a weighted average debt maturity of 6.3 years. NAREIT FFO guidance for 2021 is $1.85 to $1.95 per share, assuming an average occupancy of 95.5% to 96.5% and cash same-store NOI growth of 3% to 4%.
