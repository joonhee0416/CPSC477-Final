Despite challenges faced in 2020 due to COVID-19 and a decline in federal prison populations, The GEO Group remains optimistic about its resilience and essential government service provision. The company reported $578 million in fourth-quarter revenue, with a $0.09 per diluted share net income. However, it also recognized a $21 million goodwill impairment charge related to reentry centers impacted by the pandemic.

For 2021, GEO Group anticipates revenue between $2.24 billion and $2.27 billion, with net income per diluted share ranging from $0.88 to $0.98. Adjusted funds from operations (AFFO) are projected between $1.98 and $2.08 per diluted share. The company's guidance incorporates the potential non-renewal of additional contracts with the Bureau of Prisons (BOP) following an executive order directing against privately operated criminal detention facilities.

In response to concerns about political rhetoric and access to financing, GEO Group has reduced its quarterly dividend payment to $0.25 per share. This move aligns with the company's strategy of allocating capital towards debt repayment, aiming to reduce net debt by $75-$100 million annually. The company also continues to evaluate cost-saving opportunities and potential asset sales.

Despite the challenges, GEO Group emphasizes its commitment to operational excellence and humane care. The company highlights its successful audits and certifications, employee training, and medical services efforts. Additionally, it notes the positive response to its Continuum of Care program, which integrates in-custody rehabilitation and post-release support services. Overall, GEO Group remains confident in its ability to deliver essential government services and navigate the evolving policy landscape.
