2021 Q1 Earnings Call Summary

Huntington Ingalls Industries (HII) reported $2.3 billion in sales for the quarter, a slight increase from 2020. Diluted earnings per share were $3.68 for the quarter and $3.56, adjusted for pensions, up from $2.43 in 2020. The company also announced a record backlog of approximately $49 billion, with approximately $25 billion funded.

In terms of portfolio shaping actions, HII completed the sale of its oil and gas business and contributed the San Diego Shipyard to Titan Acquisition Holdings in exchange for a noncontrolling interest in the leading provider of ship repair and fleet sustainment services. This transaction sharpened the focus of the Technical Solutions business into areas where the company believes its unique capabilities and close customer relationships will drive strong organic revenue growth and margin expansion.

HII's Ingalls segment was awarded a life-cycle engineering and support services contract for the LPD program with a cumulative value of approximately $214 million. The team remains focused on maintaining strong cost and schedule performance in support of their planned production milestones. On the DDG program, the team is preparing for the launch of DDG 125.

The company's net earnings in the quarter were $148 million, compared to $172 million in the first quarter of 2020. Diluted earnings per share in the quarter were $3.68, compared to $4.23 in the first quarter of 2020. Excluding the impact of pension, diluted earnings per share in the quarter were $3.56, compared to $2.43 in the first quarter of 2020.

HII expects to finish the year with a strict building operating margin in the 7% to 8% range, with the significant remaining risk retirement events weighted toward the end of the year. The company also expects shipbuilding margin for the first half of 2021 to be around the midpoint of its annual guidance range.
