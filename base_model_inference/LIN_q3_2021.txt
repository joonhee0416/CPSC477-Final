Linde reported another solid quarter of record earnings per share performance, despite increasingly challenging market conditions. The company's project backlog nearly doubled, providing a strong foundation for high quality, long term growth. Linde is committed to reducing its greenhouse gas emissions footprint and has a roadmap in place to achieve this goal. The company's operating culture is focused on continuous improvement across key operating metrics. Linde's CEO, Steve, will become the Chairman of the Board, while Sanjiv will become CEO effective March first. Sanjiv has demonstrated his ability to lead the company and has been appointed through a diligent three year succession planning process. Linde's growth initiatives include securing project backlog, accretive base capex investments, and strength in low capital intensity areas such as healthcare, food and beverage, engineering, and packaged gas business. The company's project backlog has increased from $7.5 billion to $13.4 billion sequentially, and underlying sales grew 11% over prior year and 1% sequentially. Linde's operating margin of 23.6% is 150 basis points above 2020, but 60 basis points below the high mark set in the second quarter. The company's EPS of $2.73 is up 27% over last year from higher volumes and price over a relatively stable cost base. Linde has a strong productivity culture, which enables consistent profit growth, irrespective of the economic climate. The company's return on capital is 16.7%, representing another record as profit continues to grow double digit percent over a flat capital base. Linde has access to very attractive capital through the debt markets and has generated significant surplus cash. The company's full year guidance is $10.52 to $10.62, which represents a growth rate of 28% to 29% over 2020 and 43% to 45% over 2019.
