The company's revenue for the first quarter of 2021 was $640 million, up 7.3% from the same period last year. The operating earnings for the quarter were $4 million, and the net earnings were $2 million. The operating margin for the entire company was 10%, which is 70 basis points lower than the year-ago quarter. The aerospace business had a 11.7% operating margin, which is lower than the year-ago quarter, but consistent with the guidance and sell-side expectations. The Gulfstream business had a book-to-bill of 1.34:1, and the Jet Aviation business had a book-to-bill of 1.3:1. The G500 and G600 aircraft had a 5.7% increase in service revenue. The G700 aircraft is expected to enter service in the fourth quarter of 2022. The company expects the second quarter to be the most challenging from a delivery perspective, but expects rapid improvement in the third and fourth quarters. The combat systems business had revenue of $1.8 billion, up 6.6% over the year-ago quarter.
