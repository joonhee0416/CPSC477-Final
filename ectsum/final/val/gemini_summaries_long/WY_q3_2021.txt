In Q3 2021, Weyerhaeuser reported strong results despite headwinds such as weather disruptions, COVID-19, and supply chain challenges. Excluding a special gain, earnings were $0.60 per diluted share. Timberlands saw a $20 million increase in earnings, while Wood Products experienced a 60% decrease due to declining lumber and OSB prices. Real Estate and ENR contributed $45 million to earnings, while Distribution delivered $22 million of adjusted EBITDA despite lower margins. The company generated $659 million of cash from operations in Q3, bringing year-to-date total to a record $2.7 billion. Adjusted funds available for distribution (FAD) exceeded $2.4 billion year-to-date. Weyerhaeuser anticipates Q4 earnings and adjusted EBITDA for Timberlands to be comparable to Q3. Wood Products is expected to have higher earnings and adjusted EBITDA, excluding the impact of commodity price changes. Real Estate, Energy, and Natural Resources is projected to see significantly lower earnings and adjusted EBITDA in Q4 due to transaction timing.
