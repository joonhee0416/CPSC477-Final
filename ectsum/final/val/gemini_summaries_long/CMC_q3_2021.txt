CMC's third-quarter performance was exceptional, with earnings per share reaching $1.07, surpassing prior-year levels and analysts' estimates. Core EBITDA surged by 49% year-over-year, driven by increased margins and strong volume growth in both North America and Europe. The company's optimized network and strategic initiatives, including the upcoming Arizona 2 micro mill, are expected to further enhance efficiency and cost savings. Solid demand from the construction sector, particularly in the residential and infrastructure markets, boosted rebar and merchant bar shipments. Despite inflationary pressures, CMC managed to reduce controllable costs per ton, contributing to its record financial performance. The company remains optimistic about the future, citing strong bidding activity, backlog levels, and favorable market conditions. CMC also emphasizes its commitment to environmental stewardship, with plans to set ambitious sustainability goals aligned with science-based targets.
