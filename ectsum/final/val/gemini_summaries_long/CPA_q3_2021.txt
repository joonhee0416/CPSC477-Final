Copa Holdings, the parent company of Copa Airlines, reported improved financial performance in Q3 2021, driven by increased vaccination rates and reduced travel restrictions. Capacity reached 70% of pre-pandemic levels, with a load factor of 79%. Revenues increased by 46% to $445 million, while ex-fuel CASM decreased to $0.62 per ASM. The company reported an operating profit of $59 million and an operating margin of 13.3%, or 11.2% excluding passenger revenue adjustments. Copa's strong balance sheet includes $1.3 billion in cash and $1.6 billion in total liquidity. The company expects further capacity growth in Q4 and 2022, reaching 83% and 92% of 2019 levels, respectively.
