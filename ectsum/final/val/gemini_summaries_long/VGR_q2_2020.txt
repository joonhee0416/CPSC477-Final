Vector Group saw a mixed performance in Q2 2020, with a revenue increase in its tobacco segment but a significant decline in its real estate segment due to the COVID-19 pandemic. The company's cash reserves remained strong at $540 million, including deferred federal excise tax payments. Vector Group's adjusted net income was $28.7 million or $0.19 per share diluted, compared to $43.2 million or $0.28 per share in Q2 2019. The tobacco segment reported an increase of $18 million in revenues due to price increases and increased unit volume. Liggett, Vector Group's tobacco subsidiary, saw exceptional performance with a 14.5% increase in tobacco adjusted operating income due to increased unit volumes and market share. Overall, Vector Group remains confident in its market strategy and has implemented measures to mitigate the impact of COVID-19.
