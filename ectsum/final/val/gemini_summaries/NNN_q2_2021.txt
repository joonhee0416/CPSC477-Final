National Retail Properties reported strong second-quarter results, including 98.3% occupancy, 99% rent collection, and increased guidance for 2021 core FFO per share to a range of $2.75 to $2.80. The company's long-standing strategy of acquiring and leasing properties to large regional and national operators has resulted in exceptional rent collections, validating the company's focus on direct sale-leaseback transactions. Despite the pandemic, the company has maintained a strong balance sheet, with no material debt maturities until 2024 and a net debt to gross book assets ratio of 35%. National Retail Properties remains well-positioned for continued success, with a focus on growth and shareholder value. In 2021, the company celebrates 32 consecutive years of annual dividend increases, placing it among only 85 U.S. public companies with such a track record.
