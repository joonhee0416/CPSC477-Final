Despite a 26% sales decline year-over-year, the company showed resilience with a 19% sequential increase in adjusted earnings per share. Government and defense sales remain strong, contributing nearly half of total sales. Gross margin improved sequentially to 16.1%, excluding CARES Act support, due to cost reductions and efficiency gains. The company is optimistic about a market recovery as leisure travel demand increases, expecting continued margin improvement. Balance sheet strength allows for investments in growth initiatives, including digitalization, and funding for acquisitions. The company believes it will emerge from the pandemic as a stronger and more profitable enterprise.
