Eastman's first-quarter performance exceeded 2019 levels, with earnings per share up 20% and record free cash flow of $125 million, despite challenges posed by winter storm Uri. The company's circular economy initiatives have gained significant traction, with strong demand for its recycled products. Eastman continues to allocate capital strategically, including the recent acquisition of 3F Feed & Food. For the second quarter, the company anticipates sequential EPS growth and expects to meet or surpass 2018 adjusted EPS of $2.22. Full-year guidance includes adjusted EPS between $8.25 and $8.75 and free cash flow approaching $1.1 billion. Eastman attributes its resilience to its team and its focus on innovation, which has sustained growth amidst macroeconomic disruptions.
