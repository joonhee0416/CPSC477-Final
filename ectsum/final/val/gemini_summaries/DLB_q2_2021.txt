Dolby's second-quarter financial results reflected solid momentum across its business segments. Licensing revenues remained strong, with particular growth in gaming and automotive markets. The company also highlighted significant progress in expanding Dolby Vision and Dolby Atmos into new content experiences, including music and gaming. Dolby's financial performance was driven by higher-than-expected volume in multiple end markets. Despite the impact of COVID-19, cinema-related business saw initial signs of recovery. The company's technology is also gaining traction in mobile, as evidenced by the adoption of Dolby Vision and Dolby Atmos in leading smartphones. The growth in streaming content has further solidified Dolby's position within entertainment. Looking ahead, the company anticipates further adoption of its technologies, particularly in the second half of the year. Dolby expects revenue ranging from $560 million to $600 million, with Q3 revenue projected at $260 million to $290 million.
