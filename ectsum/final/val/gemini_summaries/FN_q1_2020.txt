Fabrinet reported a solid Q1, exceeding guidance with revenue of $399.3 million. Non-GAAP EPS was $0.86, also above expectations. Optical communications revenue grew 6% year-over-year to $302 million, driven by a 7% increase in telecom revenue to $230 million. Datacom revenue declined 15% to $73 million, but the company anticipates a bottoming trend and expects revenue to stabilize in Q2. Nonoptical communications revenue moderated to $97 million, with industrial laser revenue declining to $41 million. The company is establishing a second Fabrinet site in Israel to support new product introductions. Q2 guidance projects revenue between $408 million and $416 million, with non-GAAP EPS expected to be $0.91-$0.94 and GAAP EPS of $0.74-$0.77. Management remains optimistic about the company's long-term growth prospects.
