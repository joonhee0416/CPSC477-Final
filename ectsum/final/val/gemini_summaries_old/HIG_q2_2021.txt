The Hartford reported strong Q2 results with a 91% year-over-year increase in core earnings driven by higher investment income, strong commercial lines performance, and lower COVID-related losses. Property & Casualty (P&C) saw improved margins, with an 88.5 combined ratio. Commercial Lines had an underlying combined ratio of 89.4 and written premium growth of 15%. Personal Lines also had positive results with an underlying combined ratio of 88.2% and a 91.1% persistency rate. Group Benefits contributed with solid earnings, driven by favorable investment returns and improved disability trends. The company returned $694 million to shareholders through share repurchases and dividends, while also achieving an 8% increase in book value per diluted share, excluding AOCI. The company is optimistic about the future and expects to exceed its financial targets for 2022 and 2023, including a core earnings ROE of 13% to 14%.
