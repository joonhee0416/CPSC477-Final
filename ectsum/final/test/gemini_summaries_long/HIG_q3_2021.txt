The Hartford reported core earnings of $442 million or $1.26 per diluted share for the third quarter, representing a 8% increase in year-over-year diluted book value per share, excluding AOCI. Despite core earnings being impacted by Hurricane Ida, elevated pandemic-related excess mortality in Group Benefits, and the Boy Scouts of America settlement, the company maintains a strong position with an industry-leading 87.2 underlying combined ratio in Commercial Lines and a trailing 12-month core earnings ROE of 12.5%. To enhance shareholder returns, the company has increased its share repurchase program by $500 million and implemented a 10% increase in quarterly dividends. Additionally, The Hartford reported strong results in its Hartford Funds segment, with core earnings rising to $58 million, driven by a 27% increase in daily average AUM.
