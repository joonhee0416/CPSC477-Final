In Q2, Sensient Technologies reported revenue of $294.9 million, down from $302.8 million in Q2 2019. Flavors & Fragrances saw revenue growth in the mid-single digits and high-single digits in operating profit, while natural colors and pharmaceuticals also experienced growth. However, COVID-19 headwinds impacted the personal care market and regions like Latin America, Europe, and Asia-Pacific. Despite these challenges, the company expects to meet its earnings per share outlook for the year. The company has also made progress on divestitures, with the completion of the Inks business sale and a definitive agreement to sell the Yogurt Fruit Prep business. Ongoing divestitures include the aroma chemical and fragrance compound business. Consolidated adjusted operating income was $40.3 million, down from $47 million in Q2 2019. Adjusted diluted earnings per share were $0.70, compared to $0.81 in Q2 2019. The company revised its GAAP earnings per share guidance to $2.10-$2.35, which includes $0.35-$0.40 per share of divestiture and other related costs, as well as currency headwinds. On an adjusted basis, the company maintained its guidance of $2.60-$2.80, excluding these factors.
