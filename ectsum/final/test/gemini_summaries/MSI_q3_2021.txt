Q3 earnings highlighted strong demand across the business, with revenue increasing by 13% to $2.1 billion and earnings per share rising by 21% to $2.35 on a non-GAAP basis. Operating margins expanded by 150 basis points, driven by higher sales, gross margins, and operating leverage. All three technologies, LMR, video security, and access control, and command center software, witnessed growth. The company raised its full-year guidance, predicting a 10%-10.25% increase in revenue and non-GAAP earnings per share between $9 and $9.04. Despite supply chain challenges, the company maintains a record Q3 backlog of $11.4 billion. Software and services continues to drive growth and profitability, with video security and access control software revenue expected to increase by over 35% for the year. The company is well-positioned for continued growth in 2022, supported by its strong backlog, expanded relationships with existing customers, ample customer funding, and NDAA-compliant manufacturing in North America.
