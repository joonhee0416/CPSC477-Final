GameStop's sales rose by 25.6% to $1.183 billion, exceeding estimates of $1.12 billion. Despite store closures, the company expanded its fulfillment network with new facilities in Nevada and Pennsylvania, positioning itself for further growth in selection and shipping times. SG&A expenses slightly increased, but adjusted to 31.5% of sales. The company's net loss narrowed to $61.6 million from $111.3 million in the previous year's quarter, resulting in an adjusted net loss of $55 million. GameStop's balance sheet strengthened with a cash position of $1.775 billion, and it raised $1.1 billion in proceeds from an ATM offering to support growth initiatives. The company is not providing formal guidance at this time.
