H.B. Fuller reported strong third-quarter results with double-digit organic revenue growth of 16% and 13% compared to 2020 and 2019, respectively. Adjusted earnings per share were $0.79. Despite supply chain shortages and inflationary pressures, the company gained market share through innovative solutions. They have implemented $225 million in price adjustments and announced a surcharge to offset raw material cost increases, which are expected to result in over $400 million in annualized pricing revenue. Margins are expected to improve in Q4 and beyond as price increases outpace raw material increases. The company anticipates Q4 revenue growth of 15-17% and adjusted EBITDA of $460-$470 million. Despite challenges, H.B. Fuller remains well-positioned for growth with strong cash flow generation, diverse customer base, and a focus on innovation.
