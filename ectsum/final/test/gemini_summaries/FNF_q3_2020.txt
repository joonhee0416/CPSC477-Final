In the third quarter, FNF reported record adjusted pre-tax title earnings of $528 million, a 30% increase from the previous year, driven by a surge in refinance activity. The company's adjusted pre-tax title margin also reached 21.2%, a 260-basis point increase. F&G, acquired by FNF, exceeded expectations with strong sales results and the establishment of new distribution channels. The sale of F&G Reinsurance resulted in a favorable outcome for F&G and its stakeholders. FNF's capital position remains strong, with a solid RBC ratio and a modest decrease in credit impairments. The company announced a 9% increase in quarterly dividend and plans to repurchase $500 million in shares over the next 12 months. FNF's total revenue for the quarter reached $3 billion, with net earnings of $378 million and adjusted net earnings from continuing operations of $435 million, or $1.48 per diluted share.
