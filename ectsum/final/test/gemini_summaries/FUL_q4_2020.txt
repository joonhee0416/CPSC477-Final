H.B. Fuller reported strong Q4 results, with organic revenue growth of 5%, EBITDA up 9%, and EPS up 21%. This exceeded expectations, driven by share gains and cost reductions. The company's broad adhesive portfolio and diverse end market exposure contributed to its resilience. The realignment completed earlier in the year generated $30 million in annualized SG&A savings, leading to strong profit growth. H.B. Fuller continued to invest in innovation, with a strong product pipeline focused on sustainable packaging solutions and adhesives for electronics and medical applications. The company expects momentum to continue in 2021, with further share gains and margin improvement. Despite a higher expected tax rate, the company plans to pay down $200 million in debt and invest in its manufacturing network to drive efficiency and growth.
