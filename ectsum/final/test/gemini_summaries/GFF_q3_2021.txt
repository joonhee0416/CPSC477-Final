Griffon Corporation reported solid third-quarter results, exceeding expectations with a 2% revenue increase to $1.14 billion. Adjusted EBITDA reached $76 million, translating to adjusted earnings per share of $0.43. Despite supply chain disruptions and labor shortages, demand remained robust across all product categories. The company remains committed to mitigating rising costs through price adjustments and efficiency programs. Full-year guidance is reiterated, with revenue exceeding $2.5 billion and adjusted EBITDA of $320 million anticipated. The company is making progress with its AMES strategic initiative, aiming to generate cash savings of $30-$35 million and inventory reductions of the same magnitude. Griffon's strong financial position, with cash and equivalents of $221 million and available credit of $362 million, enables it to execute growth initiatives and pursue acquisition opportunities.
