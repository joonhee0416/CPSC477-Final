Phillips 66 reported impressive earnings in Q4, with adjusted earnings per share of $2.94. The company's diversified portfolio showcased resilience, with strong results in midstream, chemicals, and marketing and specialties. Notably, refining performance continued to improve for the third consecutive quarter. The company invested $1.9 billion back into the business, while returning $1.6 billion to shareholders and paying down $1.5 billion of debt. Looking ahead, Phillips 66 remains optimistic and committed to capital discipline. The 2022 capital program of $1.9 billion includes significant investments in lower carbon opportunities, aligning with the company's commitment to a sustainable future.
