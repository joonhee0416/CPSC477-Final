First Industrial reported strong Q2 results, with 98% of monthly rental billings collected in Q2 and 97% collected in July. Rent relief requests have diminished, with only $750,000 in deferrals granted to 14 tenants. The industrial sector remains resilient, with CBRE reporting 19 million square feet of net absorption and 56 million square feet of completion. Rental rates have also climbed, with overall straight-line rental rates increasing 32.4% and new leasing up 37.5%. To enhance its capital structure, First Industrial issued $300 million in senior unsecured notes at attractive rates and refinanced a $200 million term loan, securing an interest-only loan with a fixed rate of 2.49%. The company's net debt plus preferred stock to EBITDA stands at 5.2 times. Guidance for 2020 FFO and FFO before one-time items has been raised to a range of $1.76 to $1.84 per share, with a midpoint of $1.80 due to early lease-up and additional rental income.
