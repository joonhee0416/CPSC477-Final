Wolverine Worldwide experienced a robust Q1, with revenue increasing 20.1% on a reported basis and 18.2% on a constant currency basis. Saucony led the charge with an impressive 60% revenue growth and nearly 800 basis point operating margin expansion. Merrell followed closely with a 25% revenue increase, driven by strong performance in EMEA and North America. The work business also contributed significantly, with Wolverine and Cat footwear growth exceeding 30%. Sperry revenue declined slightly, but showed sequential improvement and a strong full-price business. The company raised its full-year guidance based on strong demand and a healthy order book, projecting revenue growth of 25-28% to $2.24-$2.30 billion. Adjusted diluted earnings per share are now expected to range from $1.95 to $2.10.
