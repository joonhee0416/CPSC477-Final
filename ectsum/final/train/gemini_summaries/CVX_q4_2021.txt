In 2021, Chevron achieved record free cash flow, surpassing its previous high by 25%. The company successfully integrated Noble Energy, doubling initial synergy estimates. Notably, Chevron accelerated its lower carbon strategy by establishing a net zero aspiration and introducing a portfolio carbon intensity target. Financially, Chevron recorded its highest earnings since 2014 and increased its dividend for the 34th consecutive year. Despite flat to down production anticipated in 2022 due to contract expirations, Chevron reaffirmed its long-term production growth target of 3% CAGR through 2025. The company expects higher cash returns to shareholders through dividend increases and share buybacks, relying on its strong balance sheet and ongoing focus on cost efficiency. Overall, Chevron continues to optimize its operations, drive higher returns, and lead the transition to a lower carbon future.
