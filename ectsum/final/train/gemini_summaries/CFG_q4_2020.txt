Citizens Bank delivered a strong fourth quarter and full-year performance in 2020. The bank's underlying earnings per share rose to $1.04 in Q4, driven by strong mortgage and capital markets businesses. Citizens also announced a $750 million share repurchase authorization. For 2021, the bank expects revenue, expenses, and PPNR to be slightly higher than consensus estimates. Credit metrics are trending favorably, with a projected NCO range of 50-65 basis points and a reserve release expected due to improving economic conditions. Despite a forecast decline in fee income from its record 2020 levels, Citizens projects loan growth in the mid to high single digits and net charge-offs of 50-65 basis points. The bank's strong capital and reserve position of 2.24% ACL ratio and 10% CET1 ratio provides flexibility for investment in strategic initiatives and loan growth.
