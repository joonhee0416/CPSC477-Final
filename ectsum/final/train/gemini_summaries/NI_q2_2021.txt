NiSource reported non-GAAP diluted net operating earnings of $0.13 per share in Q2, reaffirming its earnings guidance of $1.32 to $1.36 per share for 2021. Despite impacts from COVID-19, the company made progress in its generation transition with renewable projects approved in Indiana. It continues to expect 7-9% annual growth in diluted net operating earnings per share from 2021 to 2024, driven by infrastructure programs and generation investments. Environmental performance remains a priority, with a target of reducing greenhouse gas emissions by 90% by 2030, achieving a 39% reduction in pipeline methane emissions to date. Safety advancements include implementing an industry-leading safety management system and deploying advanced leak detection technology. NiSource filed rate cases in Ohio, Kentucky, Maryland, and Pennsylvania to support infrastructure investments and modernization efforts. The Ohio case seeks a $221 million annual revenue increase, while the Kentucky and Maryland cases request $27 million and $5 million, respectively. The Indiana Utility Regulatory Commission has approved 13 of NiSource's 14 proposed renewable energy projects. The company has received over 180 proposals for its 2021 integrated resource plan, which is expected to drive further generation replacement strategy in Indiana.
