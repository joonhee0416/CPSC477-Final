Revenue declined 24% year-over-year on an adjusted basis in Q3 2020 while Protiviti continued to show gains, reporting its 12th consecutive quarter of year-on-year revenue increases. This resulted in earnings per share of $0.67 compared to $1.01 in Q3 2019. Nonetheless, sequential gains were recognized across divisions with September revenue down 29.3% and 30.1% for temporary/consultant staffing and permanent placement respectively, compared to the prior year. In the first two weeks of October, revenues were down 27% and 31% respectively. Protiviti saw a 6% increase in revenue on an adjusted basis, with revenue growth of 5% to 7% expected in Q4 2020. The company remains optimistic, citing growth in the partnership between Protiviti and staffing operations, strength in services to the public sector and financial services clients, increased demand for services in cloud technology and digital transformation, as well as improving trends in the small business community.
