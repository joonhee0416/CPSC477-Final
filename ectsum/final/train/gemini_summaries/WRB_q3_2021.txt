Driven by strong underwriting results, net investment income, and foreign currency gains, the company reported a 100% increase in operating income to $247 million, resulting in $1.32 earnings per share. Gross premiums written grew by 23.2% to a record $2.8 billion, with net premiums written increasing by 23.7% to $2.3 billion. Despite current accident year catastrophe losses of $74 million (3.5 loss ratio points), underwriting profits rose by 80% to $200 million, driven by rate improvements and a favorable business mix. The current accident year combined ratio, excluding catastrophes, was 86.9%. Net investment income increased by 26.1% to $180 million, primarily due to strong results in investment funds. The company also repurchased approximately 1.3 million shares at an average price of $72.03 per share, resulting in a 1.5% increase in book value per share. The company's strong performance led to a 16.6% return on beginning-of-year equity, reflecting a healthy overall quarter for the organization.
