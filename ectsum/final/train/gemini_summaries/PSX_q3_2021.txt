In Q3, the company saw a significant improvement in earnings and cash generation. Adjusted earnings were $1.4 billion, or $3.18 per share, driven by contributions from Refining, Midstream, Chemicals, and Marketing and Specialties. Operating cash flow reached $2.2 billion, which exceeded capital spending and dividends. The company announced an acquisition of Phillips 66 Partners, simplifying its corporate structure. In Midstream, the Sweeny Hub construction is approximately one-third complete, and the C2G pipeline is operational. Chemicals reported record earnings in Olefins and Polyolefins, while Marketing and Specialties posted its second-best quarter ever. Despite the Alliance Refinery shutdown due to Hurricane Ida, the company is progressing on the Rodeo Renewed project, which will produce renewable fuels upon completion. It is also advancing initiatives in renewable fuels, batteries, carbon capture, and hydrogen through the Emerging Energy Group. The company remains committed to disciplined capital allocation, strong returns, and its role in the energy transition.
