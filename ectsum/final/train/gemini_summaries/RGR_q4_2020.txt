In 2020, the company's net sales increased by 39% to $568.9 million, resulting in a diluted earnings increase of $3.27 per share to $5.09. The surge in demand contributed to a 61% increase in fourth-quarter sales, reaching $169.3 million and yielding diluted earnings of $1.78 per share, up from $0.46 in the same period in 2019. Despite the COVID-19 pandemic, the company implemented safety measures to mitigate its impact and experienced expense reductions. Notably, the company invested $28 million in acquiring Marlin Firearms assets and plans to begin shipping Marlin lever-action rifles in late 2021. With a strong financial position, including $141.2 million in cash and short-term investments and no debt, the company has returned $114 million to shareholders through dividends and stock repurchases in 2020.
