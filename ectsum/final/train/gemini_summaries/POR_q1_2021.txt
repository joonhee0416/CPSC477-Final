The company reported strong financial results in Q1, with a net income of $96 million and earnings per share of $1.07, surpassing last year's $81 million and $0.91 per share, respectively. Despite significant weather-related challenges, revenues increased by 1.2% on a weather-adjusted basis and 2.3% after accounting for the harsh winter conditions. Industrial usage experienced an impressive 8% growth, driven by the expansion of digital companies and the global semiconductor shortage. The company reaffirmed its 2021 earnings guidance of $2.55 to $2.70 per share. The cumulative incurred cost from the February storm is estimated at $45 million net, and an application for cost deferral has been filed. Capital expenditures have increased by $45 million, most of which is related to storm restoration efforts. The company maintained a solid balance sheet with strong liquidity, investment-grade ratings, and a stable outlook. A 5.5% dividend increase was approved, consistent with its long-term dividend growth guidance of 5% to 7%.
