Simpson experienced a strong second quarter despite economic challenges due to COVID-19, reporting a 7% year-over-year sales increase to $326.1 million. The company's gross profit margin remained strong at 45.9%, and effective expense management led to a 35% increase in income from operations to $72.2 million. Notable factors driving performance include a new customer relationship with Lowe's, increased demand from the home remodeling market, and operational safety protocols that allowed for continued business amidst the pandemic. The company's net income rose to $53.5 million, translating to $1.22 per diluted share, and its balance sheet remains healthy with ample liquidity. Simpson anticipates a 1.5%-4% increase in net sales for the full fiscal year 2020, with gross margins ranging from 43% to 45%.
