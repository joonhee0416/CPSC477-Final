Franklin Street Properties Corp reported FFO of $0.16 per share for Q4 2020, experiencing a one-time charge of $3.1 million due to tenant bankruptcy. Total write-offs and lost rent amounted to $3.8 million, or 1.5% of annual rental income. Despite pandemic challenges, the company secured tenant rent deferrals, with $200,000 impacting GAAP and FFO in 2020. The company's liquidity stood at $601 million at year-end. Key priorities for 2021 include navigating COVID-19 with tenants, leasing vacancies, pursuing additional property dispositions estimated at $350-$450 million, and maintaining a focus on Sunbelt and Mountain West markets. These dispositions aim to repay debt and enhance financial flexibility. FSP is actively tracking prospective leases and renewals worth 700,000 square feet, with potential net absorption of approximately 200,000 square feet in the next 3-6 months.
