In Q3 2021, the company faced significant inflation, with labor inflation reaching 8.7%. Despite these challenges, the business performed well, generating $1.2 billion in cash from operations, putting it on track to meet its financial outlook. The company's pricing programs delivered a 4.6% core price, with standout performance in the residential line of business (5% yield) and MSW (3.5% yield). To combat cost inflation, the company will focus on disciplined pricing, which has resulted in their lowest level of rollbacks in over a decade. The company is also addressing labor challenges directly and exploring automation opportunities. With continued strength in volume growth, recycled commodity prices, and RIN values, the company has raised its revenue outlook to between 17% and 17.5% for the year. Adjusted operating EBITDA is now expected to be between $5.0 billion and $5.1 billion, an increase of about 17% at the midpoint.
