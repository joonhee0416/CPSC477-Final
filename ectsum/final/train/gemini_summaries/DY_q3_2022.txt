Revenue rose 6.6% to $854 million, driven by increased demand from large telecom customers. Gross margin declined due to project complexity, but adjusted EBITDA and EPS reached $83.1 million (9.7% of revenue) and $0.95, respectively. The company repaid convertible notes and received $500 million in construction service awards after the quarter ended. Fiber network deployments are increasing in rural areas due to government funding and industry initiatives. The company is prioritizing organic growth and opportunistic share repurchases. Operating cash flow was strong at $104.3 million, resulting in improved liquidity of $314.7 million. Despite macroeconomic headwinds, the company expects modest revenue growth and in-line to slightly higher adjusted EBITDA as a percentage of contract revenues in Q4 compared to Q4 '21.
