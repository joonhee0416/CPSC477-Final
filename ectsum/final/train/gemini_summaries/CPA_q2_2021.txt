Copa Holdings reported a net profit of $28.1 million or $0.66 per share in Q2, driven by improved load factors, increased yields, and capacity growth to 48% of pre-COVID levels. Excluding special items, the company would have reported a net loss of $16.2 million or $0.38 per share. Despite operating at reduced capacity, Copa saw its ex-fuel CASM improve to $0.076 per ASM. The company ended the quarter with $1.3 billion in cash and total liquidity of $1.6 billion. Looking ahead to Q3, Copa expects capacity to reach approximately 70% of 2019 levels, with revenues forecast to be around 58% of Q3 2019 levels. The company expects to be cash neutral in Q3, assuming an all-in fuel price of $2.15 per gallon and additional capex for fleet reactivation.
