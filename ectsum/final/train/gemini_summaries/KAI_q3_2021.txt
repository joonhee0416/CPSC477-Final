Despite supply chain constraints and increased costs, the company experienced record revenue, bookings, and EBITDA margin performance in the third quarter. Aftermarket parts and consumables revenue surged 28% to $131 million, driven by customer demand. The company made three acquisitions during the quarter, further boosting revenue. While revenue is expected to be lower than anticipated in the fourth quarter due to supply chain headwinds, full-year revenue is still projected to be between $778 and $783 million. The company has a solid financial position with increased cash flow and liquidity, despite higher net debt. Bookings remain strong with a record quarter-end backlog of $299 million. Overall, the company is well-positioned for a strong finish to the year and beyond, despite the current economic challenges.
