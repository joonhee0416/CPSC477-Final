The company reported strong third-quarter financial results, with record adjusted EBITDA of $73.9 million. Cokemaking operations ran at full capacity, while the Logistics segment delivered a solid quarter despite Hurricane Ida's disruptions, recovering quickly with minimal impact on business. The company's export and foundry coke initiatives are performing well, benefiting from positive market dynamics and establishing the company as a reliable supplier. Domestic Coke operations saw higher volumes and profitability, with full-year adjusted EBITDA expected to exceed guidance. The Logistics business generated higher revenue driven by increased volumes and a coal handling price adjustment. The company maintains a strong liquidity position with $291 million and plans to continue paying down its revolver. Gross leverage stands at approximately 2.5 times, and the company aims to modestly exceed its full-year 2021 adjusted EBITDA guidance of $255 million to $265 million based on strong performance and market strength.
