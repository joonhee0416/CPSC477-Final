Fabrinet reported strong Q2 results, with record quarterly revenue of $426.2 million and non-GAAP net income of $1 per share, exceeding guidance. Optical communications revenue grew 6% year-over-year to $322 million, driven by telecom growth. Non-optical communications business also performed well, with industrial laser revenue coming in at $46 million. The company anticipates year-over-year growth in Q3 but expects revenue to moderate due to the coronavirus outbreak in China. Despite this near-term impact, the company remains optimistic about its long-term prospects. In other news, Csaba Sverha has been appointed as the new CFO, succeeding TS Ng, who remains committed to a smooth transition.
