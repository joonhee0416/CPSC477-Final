In the face of COVID-19 challenges, CTS reported a 30% decline in sales compared to Q2 2019, with a significant drop in transportation sales. Despite the downturn, CTS delivered an adjusted EBITDA margin of 16.7% and adjusted earnings per share of $0.16. The company expects a prolonged recovery and is implementing a restructuring plan over the next two years to realign costs. The restructuring is projected to lead to annualized earnings per share improvement of $0.22 to $0.26 by the second half of 2022. CTS remains focused on strategic growth, securing new business awards, and adding 13 new customers in the quarter. Despite the challenges, CTS ended the quarter with $146 million in cash, $141 million in debt, and has access to $157 million in revolving credit to ensure liquidity.
