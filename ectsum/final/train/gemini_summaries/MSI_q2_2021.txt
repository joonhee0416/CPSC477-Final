In Q2, the company reported a 22% revenue increase, a 49% EPS increase, and an 86% operating cash flow increase year-over-year. Both segments saw robust demand, with Products and Systems Integration revenue rising 24%, driven by LMR and video security, while Software and Services revenue grew 19%, boosted by LMR services, video security, and command center software. Non-GAAP operating margins increased by 24.4% due to higher sales and leverage. The company raised its full-year guidance for sales and earnings per share based on a solid backlog and ongoing momentum. They now anticipate a 9.5% to 10% sales increase and non-GAAP earnings per share of $8.88 to $8.98, up from their previous estimates. The company remains encouraged by their execution, particularly in video security and command center software, and is optimistic about future growth despite ongoing supply chain challenges.
