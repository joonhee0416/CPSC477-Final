Church & Dwight experienced a strong Q3, driven by increased consumer demand for its products amid the pandemic. Reported sales grew 13.9%, with organic sales rising 9.9%. Notably, gummy vitamins, baking soda, and laundry products saw double-digit consumption growth. E-commerce sales also surged, with a 77% increase in Q3 and an expected 13% contribution to full-year sales. Despite ongoing challenges with supply chain costs, the company raised its full-year sales growth outlook to 11% and organic sales growth to 9%. Church & Dwight remains optimistic about the future, focusing on health and wellness trends and investing in new capacity. In 2021, the company anticipates continued momentum and adjusted earnings per share growth of 13%-14%.
