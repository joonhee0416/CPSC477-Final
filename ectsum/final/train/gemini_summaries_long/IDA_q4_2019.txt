IDACORP's financial performance in 2019 was strong, with quarterly earnings per share of $0.93, an increase of $0.41 from the previous year. For the full year, earnings per share were $4.61, up $0.12 from 2018, marking 12 consecutive years of earnings growth. Employee safety, customer satisfaction, and reliability all set records. Idaho Power's customer base grew 2.5% in 2019, with 2.7% growth in the residential segment. Idaho's GDP is forecast to grow 4.4% in both 2020 and 2021. The Boardman to Hemingway transmission project made progress, with a proposed order for the 300-mile line expected this year. Idaho Power plans to invest 120 megawatts of solar from the Jackpot Solar power purchase agreement. No general rate case is expected in Idaho or Oregon in the next 12 months. For 2020, IDACORP anticipates earnings per share in the range of $4.45 to $4.65, assuming no use of additional tax credits. O&M expenses are expected to remain flat at $350 million to $360 million, while capital expenditures are forecast to increase slightly to $300 million to $310 million.
