NiSource reported non-GAAP net operating earnings of $0.13 per share for Q2 2021, reaffirming its earnings guidance of $1.32-$1.36 per share for the year. The company plans to invest $1.9-$2.2 billion annually in safety and modernization and approximately $2 billion in renewables and transmission through 2023. It expects 7% to 9% compound annual growth in diluted net operating earnings per share from 2021 through 2024 and a 5% to 7% annual growth through 2023. NiSource has received Indiana Commission approval for all its joint venture renewable projects and filed 180 proposals in its 2021 integrated resource plan (IRP) process. The company's ongoing generation transition will replace retiring capacity and provide substantial cost savings for customers. NiSource remains committed to environmental goals and is on track to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by 90% by 2030 compared to 2005 levels.
