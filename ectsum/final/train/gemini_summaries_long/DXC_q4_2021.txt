DXC Technology reported strong Q4 results, exceeding revenue, adjusted EBIT margin, and EPS guidance. Revenue reached $4.39 billion, driven by customer investments and cost optimization programs. Adjusted EBIT margin improved to 7.5%, expanding for the third straight quarter. The book-to-bill ratio remained above 1.0 for the fourth consecutive quarter, with 53% of bookings coming from new work. DXC plans to continue its transformation journey by investing in customers, optimizing costs, and seizing market opportunities. For FY '22, they expect organic revenue growth of -1% to -2%, an adjusted EBIT margin of 8.2% to 8.7%, and non-GAAP diluted EPS of $3.45 to $3.65. Over the longer term, DXC aims for positive organic revenue growth, an adjusted EBIT margin of 10% to 11%, and non-GAAP diluted EPS of $5 to $5.25. The company emphasized its commitment to building a strong financial foundation, reducing debt, and improving cash flow.
