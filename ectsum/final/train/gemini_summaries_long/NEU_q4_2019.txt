Petroleum additives continued to perform steadily in Q4 2019, with sales decreasing 1% to $532 million. However, operating profit declined 7.4% to $73.6 million due to changes in selling prices and foreign currency rates, although it was partially offset by lower raw material costs. Despite this, the full-year operating profit for petroleum additives increased by 15.5% to $359 million. The company's net income for Q4 2019 was $50.1 million or $4.48 per share, while the income tax expense rose to $16.5 million due to the Tax Reform Act. The company's petroleum additives operating profit for the rolling four quarters ended December 31, 2019, was 16.5%, indicating a return to historical ranges. The company is comfortable maintaining a net debt to EBITDA in the 1.5 to 2 times range. Capital investments are expected to be in the $75 million to $85 million range in 2020. The company will continue to focus on margin emphasis, research and development, and reinvestment in the business while rewarding shareholders through dividends and stock buybacks.
