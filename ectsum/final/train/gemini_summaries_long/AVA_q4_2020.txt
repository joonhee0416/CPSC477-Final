Avista's earnings, which exceeded expectations, amounted to $0.85 per diluted share in Q4 2020, an improvement over the $0.76 reported in Q4 2019. Notably, for 2020, earnings reached $1.90 per diluted share, despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic. The company's smart meter deployment project has nearly reached completion in Washington, providing customers with real-time energy data and enabling proactive alerts to avoid bill surprises. Avista's Rattlesnake Flat Wind Farm, with its 57 turbines, commenced operations in December 2020, generating 50 megawatts of renewable energy. The company has implemented a comprehensive wildfire resiliency plan with an investment of $330 million over its 10-year span. Recognized as one of the World's Most Ethical Companies in 2021, Avista has raised its dividend for the 19th consecutive year. The company anticipates investing $415 million in capital expenditures in 2021, with a focus on maintaining and modernizing its infrastructure. Earnings guidance for 2021-2023 has been initiated, with ranges set at $1.96 to $2.16, $2.18 to $2.38, and $2.42 to $2.62 per diluted share, respectively.
