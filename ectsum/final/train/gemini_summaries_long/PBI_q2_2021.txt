Pitney Bowes delivered a solid second quarter, demonstrating growth across all businesses. Overall revenue increased 6% and EBIT improved 16% at constant currency. Presort and SendTech businesses both grew revenue and EBIT, with Presort volumes recovering towards pre-pandemic levels and SendTech benefiting from its SendPro product family and SaaS-based shipping portfolio. Global e-commerce turned EBITDA positive and saw improved EBIT despite a tough comparison, indicating progress toward profitability. Despite facing supply chain challenges, equipment sales grew 46%. Notably, the company made significant progress on unit economics in e-commerce, particularly in labor and transportation costs. The company's full-year outlook remains unchanged, with revenue expected to grow in the low to mid-single-digit range and adjusted earnings per share projected to be between $0.35 and $0.42.
