Revenue of $222 million and earnings per share of $0.85 marked a solid start to the year for the company. Machine Clothing had a strong quarter, with its highest revenue since 2015. Orders were down compared to last year, but the backlog is stronger, and guidance remains in the range of $570 million to $590 million. Engineered Composites is facing destocking in Boeing 787 and LEAP programs, with defense programs now comprising over 48% of revenue. Despite these challenges, revenue and adjusted EBITDA guidance remains unchanged at $275 million to $295 million and $55 million to $65 million, respectively. Overall, the company's financial performance is meeting expectations, with free cash flow improving and a healthy balance sheet enabling investment in growth initiatives. The company remains optimistic about long-term opportunities in both segments.
