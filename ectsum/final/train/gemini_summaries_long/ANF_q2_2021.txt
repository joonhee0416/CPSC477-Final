In Q2, abercrombie and fitch reported strong results, exceeding revenue expectations with a 24% increase in sales to $865 million compared to last year. Adjusted earnings per share reached $1.70, highlighting the company's operational efficiency. Notably, digital sales remained steady, contributing 44% of total sales. The denim category witnessed a significant surge, with new styles driving sales and reducing the need for promotions. The company is optimistic about the ongoing back-to-school season in the US, expecting it to extend due to delayed reopenings in international markets. Despite industry-wide supply chain disruptions and cost pressures, abercrombie and fitch expects to exceed its previously stated 5.8% operating margin goal for the year. The company remains committed to investing in growth initiatives, including marketing, digital experiences, and the expansion of its Gilly Hicks and Social Tourist brands.
