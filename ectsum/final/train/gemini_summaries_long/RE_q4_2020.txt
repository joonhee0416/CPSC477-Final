In 2020, Everest achieved solid results despite challenges, including COVID-19. The company recorded $514 million in net income, grew gross written premiums by 15%, and improved its underlying combined ratio to 87.5%. Reinsurance saw strong growth and profitability, while insurance delivered record renewal rate increases and an improved combined ratio. The company took decisive actions, including reserve strengthening and COVID-19 loss provisions. Everest maintained a strong capital position and focused on expense management, with a low expense ratio of 5.8%. The company's investments contributed to earnings, with pre-tax investment income of $642 million. Everest is well-positioned for 2021, with a focus on diversifying its business, improving risk-adjusted returns, and delivering shareholder value.
