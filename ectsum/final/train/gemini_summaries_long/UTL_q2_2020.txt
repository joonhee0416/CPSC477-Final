In its second quarter, the company reported a net income of $3.1 million, a $0.9 million decrease compared to 2019, attributed to COVID-19 and warm weather. The financial impact of COVID-19 led to an estimated $0.4 million decrease in earnings per share. Gas gross margin declined by $0.4 million due to lower commercial and industrial usage and early summer weather impacts. Electric gross margin remained flat, with an increase in margins offset by lower commercial and industrial usage during COVID-19. Despite the pandemic, the company increased capital spending by $10 million compared to 2019. The company also secured $95 million in long-term debt, providing ample liquidity for growth plans. Rate case activity in Maine and Massachusetts concluded, resulting in revenue increases of $3.6 million and $4.6 million, respectively. The consolidated return on equity was 8.4% in the last 12 months, with an estimated 9.3% after weather normalization.
