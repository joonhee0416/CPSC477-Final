First-quarter earnings exceeded expectations with a recurring GAAP EPS of $0.79, driven by reserve releases and PPP fees. Nonbanking businesses saw strong revenue and margin growth, with benefits, wealth management, and insurance businesses showing notable increases. Loan growth remained steady, and loan quality improved significantly. Margin contraction remains a concern, and the commercial pipeline needs to be rebuilt. The company's liquidity position remains strong with over $5.67 billion of available liquidity. The Company's focus for the rest of the year includes credit loss mitigation, new loan generation, and excess liquidity deployment while navigating continued net interest margin pressures.
