Skechers had an exceptional second quarter with record sales of $1.66 billion, exceeding estimates and rising 32% compared to 2019. International sales comprised 56% of total sales, led by strong growth in China. Direct-to-consumer sales also surged by 138%, driven by a 17% increase in average selling price. Despite ongoing pandemic challenges, Skechers saw improvements in key markets like the UK, Germany, and Mexico. The company's innovative comfort footwear, strategic marketing, and infrastructure expansion have resonated with consumers seeking comfort and style. Looking ahead, Skechers anticipates strong performance in the third quarter and full year, with sales and earnings per share exceeding previous guidance. The company remains optimistic about its global expansion and the continued growth of its e-commerce business.
