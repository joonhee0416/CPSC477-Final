Stewart Title Company reported strong second-quarter earnings, driven by a 50% increase in total title revenues to $802 million. Residential agency and commercial operations led the revenue surge, with residential revenues increasing 47% and commercial revenues rising 97%. Despite increased title revenues, title loss expense rose 56%, reflecting a 4.5% loss expense ratio. The company's pre-tax margin for the title segment improved to 17%, compared to 11% in Q2 2020. Operating expenses increased due to higher revenue and order activity, but improved as a percentage of operating revenues. Stewart's financial position remains solid, with cash and investments exceeding regulatory requirements by $600 million and available credit facility of $225 million. Total open orders increased 8%, while closed orders improved 27%. The company remains optimistic about the real estate market and is focused on gaining scale, improving service, and expanding digital capabilities.
