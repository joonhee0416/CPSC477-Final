In fiscal Q1 2021, despite ongoing challenges from COVID-19, the company reported a strong start, exceeding expectations. Sales reached $266 million, close to 2019 levels, driven by impressive growth in e-commerce and restaurants. Gross margins expanded significantly, resulting in improved profitability across all operating groups. The company's focus on delivering happiness to customers, investing in digital and store capabilities, and enhancing brand messages has strengthened its foundation for profitable growth. As restrictions ease and regions recover, the company anticipates continued momentum in the coming quarters. Despite the closure of Lanier Apparel, overall sales are projected to range between $1.015 billion to $1.05 billion in 2021, with adjusted earnings per share exceeding 2019 levels. Plans include ongoing investments in growth opportunities, including IT enhancements, new store openings, and the launch of a new Marlin Bar.
