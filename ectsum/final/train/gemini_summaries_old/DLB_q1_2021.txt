Dolby Laboratories reported strong first quarter financials, exceeding guidance with a revenue of $390 million, driven by increased licensing revenue from adoption of Dolby technologies and cinema revenue recovery. Despite ongoing COVID-19 uncertainty, the company anticipates continued growth in the second half of FY '21, driven by higher Dolby adoption and recovery in the cinema space.

For Q2, Dolby projects revenue to range from $280 million to $310 million. Licensing revenue is estimated to comprise $270 million to $295 million, while products and services revenue is expected to range from $10 million to $15 million.

Earnings per share are forecast to range from $0.36 to $0.51 on a GAAP basis and $0.57 to $0.72 on a non-GAAP basis.

Beyond fiscal Q2, Dolby sees opportunities for growth in music, gaming, and virtual experiences through its Dolby.io platform. The combined Dolby Vision and Dolby Atmos experience continues to gain traction across various content and devices, reinforcing Dolby's position in delivering immersive entertainment experiences.
