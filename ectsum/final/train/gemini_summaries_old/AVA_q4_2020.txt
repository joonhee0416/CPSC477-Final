In Q4 2020, Avista reported solid earnings, surpassing expectations with $0.85 per diluted share compared to $0.76 in Q4 2019. Full-year earnings were $1.90 per diluted share, down from $2.97 in 2019. Despite the COVID-19 pandemic, the company made operational progress, completing the installation of smart meters and natural gas modules in Washington. This technology provides customers with real-time data and enables the company to proactively respond to potential outages. Avista also invested in clean energy with the completion of the Rattlesnake Flat Wind Farm, generating 50 megawatts of renewable energy for customers. To enhance wildfire resiliency, the company implemented a comprehensive 10-year plan, allocating $330 million over its lifespan. Avista's commitment to ethical business practices earned it recognition as one of the World's Most Ethical Companies by Ethisphere for the second consecutive year. Looking ahead, the company remains focused on investing in utility infrastructure and providing safe, reliable energy at an affordable cost. Earnings guidance for 2021-2023 ranges from $1.96 to $2.62 per diluted share, aiming to achieve its allowed return by 2023. With timely rate relief, Avista projects a post-2023 earnings growth rate of 4% to 6%.
