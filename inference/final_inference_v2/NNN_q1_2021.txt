 1. Revenue and Earnings

The company's portfolio of 3,161 freestanding single-tenant retail properties performed well in the first quarter. Occupancy was 98.3% at the end of the quarter, which is above the long-term average of 98%. The company collected 97% of rents due for the first quarter and 98% of rents due for the month of April. The company's impressive collection results continue to compare favorably to other retail real estate companies, including those with a significantly higher percentage of investment-grade tenants. The majority of the remaining uncollected rent in the first quarter was simply deferred rent that will be collected when the tenant's repayment obligation kicks in later this year. The company only forgave 0.1% of its first quarter rents.

# 2. Occupancy and Leasing

The company's occupancy rate ticked down 20 basis points from December 31, but the company is seeing impressive activity in its leasing department, including interest by a number of strong national tenants in some of its vacancies. The company recently announced collection of 97% of rents due for the first quarter as well as collection of 98% of rents due for the month of April.

# 3. Capital Structure

The company ended the first quarter with $311 million of cash in the bank, a 0 balance on its $900 million line of credit, no material debt maturities until 2024 and an average debt duration of over 13 years. The company is well-positioned to fund all of its 2021 acquisition guidance with the available capital on hand.
