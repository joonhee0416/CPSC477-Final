 2021 Q1 Earnings Call Summary

The company's revenue for the first quarter of 2021 was $640 million, up 7.3% from the same period last year. The operating earnings for the quarter were $4 million, and the net earnings were $2 million. The operating margin for the entire company was 10%, which is 70 basis points lower than the year-ago quarter.

The aerospace division had revenue of $1.9 billion and operating earnings of $220 million with an 11.7% operating margin. The Gulfstream business had a book-to-bill ratio of 1.34:1 and a 5.7% increase in service revenue. The jet aviation business had a book-to-bill ratio of 1.3:1 and a 31 million decrease in service revenue. The G500 and G600 aircraft continued to perform well, with margins improving on a consistent basis.

The company expects the second quarter to be the most challenging from a delivery perspective, with rapid improvement in the third and fourth quarters. The G700 aircraft is expected to enter service in the fourth quarter of 2022. The combat systems division had revenue of $1.8 billion, up 6.6% from the year-ago quarter.

The company's cash performance in the quarter was relatively strong, with free cash flow conversion expected to be in the 95% to 100% range for the year. The company expects order activity and backlog to continue to be strong, with a 1:1 book-to-bill ratio for the company as a whole.
