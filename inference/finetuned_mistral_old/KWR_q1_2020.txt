"Quaker Houghton, a chemical company, has reported its first quarter 2020 earnings. The company has estimated that COVID-19 impacted its sales by about 4% in the first quarter, and the two main drivers of this decline were the reduction in production in the automotive sector and the temporary halt of 737 MAX production by Boeing. Despite this, the company has achieved a total organic volume growth of approximately 2% in the first quarter of 2020 compared to the same period in 2019.

The company's gross margins have also improved, with the combined gross margins of Quaker Houghton expected to be about 1% lower than stand-alone Quaker, compared to the previously expected 1%. The company has achieved this improvement through cost savings from its integration, the impact of the Norman Hay acquisition, and additional cost control measures implemented to combat the global effect of COVID-19 on volumes.
