2021 Earnings Call Summary

The company announced an increase in its 2021 core FFO guidance from a range of $2.55 to $2.62 per share to a range of $2.70 to $2.75 per share. The increase was due to better-than-expected rent collections and the results from Q1. The company's portfolio of 3,161 freestanding single-tenant retail properties continued to perform well, with occupancy at 98.3% at the end of the quarter, which is above the long-term average of 98%. The company collected 97% of rents due for the first quarter and 98% of rents due for the month of April, which is significantly higher than other retail real estate companies. The company only forgave 0.1% of its first quarter rents. The company acquired 29 new properties in the quarter for just under $106 million, at an initial cash cap rate of 6.4% and with an average lease duration of 17.5 years.
