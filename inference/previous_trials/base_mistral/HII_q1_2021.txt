2021 Q1 Earnings Call Summary

Huntington Ingalls Industries (HII) reported $2.3 billion in sales for the quarter, a slight increase from 2020. Diluted earnings per share were $3.68 for the quarter and $3.56, adjusted for pensions, up from $2.43 in 2020. The company also announced a record backlog of approximately $49 billion, with approximately $25 billion funded.

In terms of portfolio shaping actions, HII completed the sale of its oil and gas business and contributed the San Diego Shipyard to Titan Acquisition Holdings in exchange for a noncontrolling interest in the leading provider of ship repair and fleet sustainment services. This transaction sharpened the focus of the Technical Solutions business into areas where the company believes its unique capabilities and close customer relationships will drive strong organic revenue growth and margin expansion.
