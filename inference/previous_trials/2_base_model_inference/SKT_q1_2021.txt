 

During the earnings call, Tanger Outlets discussed their financial performance for the first quarter of 2021. They are encouraged by the greater macro outlook over the past 90 days as vaccination rollout continues and an improving retail environment. The improvement in some of their operating metrics reflects the excellent value proposition that their open-air centers provide for both retailers and shoppers. They are focused on rebuilding their occupancy, driving leasing, and curating their merchandise mix to maximize shopper frequency and dwell time, and to bring new customers to Tanger Centers. Their consolidated portfolio occupancy was 91.7% at the end of the quarter, up only 20 basis points from the end of 2020. Blended average rental rates decreased 2.8% on a straight-line basis and 8.5% on a cash basis for all renewals and retenanted leases that commenced during the trailing 12 months ended March 31, 2021. They believe they will continue to see improvement longer-term as positive traffic and sales trends will support driving better rents. However, in the near term, they anticipate that they will continue to see pressure on retenanting spreads this year as they fill recaptured space that was at rental rates above the portfolio average.
