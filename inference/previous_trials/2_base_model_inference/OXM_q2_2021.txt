 2020 Earnings Call Summary

The company's performance was strong across all brands, particularly in direct-to-consumer channels of distribution, with a 22% increase in combine to direct-to-consumer, full price store, and e-commerce comp growth in the second quarter compared to 2019. The company's focus on improving the customer experience has resulted in a 15% growth in the biggest brand, Tommy Bahama, and a remarkable 31% growth in Lilly Pulitzer. The company's e-commerce business has been enhanced by its ship from store capability, which allows it to leverage inventories located in stores to serve e-commerce demand. The company expects its total e-commerce business to be roughly a third of consolidated sales in 2021, compared to 23% in fiscal 2019. The company's retail stores grew top line during the quarter, modestly over 2019 levels, despite lower traffic. The company's liquidity position is strong with $180 million in cash and no borrowings outstanding under its revolving credit facility at the end of the second quarter.
