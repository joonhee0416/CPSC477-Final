 

In the earnings call, the company discussed its financial results for the fourth quarter of 2019 and provided updates on its insurance portfolios. The company's main priority continues to be remediation of its Puerto Rico credits, which accounted for approximately half of its total insured Puerto Rico debt service at year-end 2019. The company's remaining Puerto Rico exposure is largely comprised of three Puerto Rico credits: the Commonwealth pre-2011 General Obligation PBA bonds; the Puerto Rico Electric Power Authority or PREPA; and the Puerto Rico Highways & Transportation Authority or HTA. The company's insured portfolio declined to $49 billion of gross par outstanding, down $9 billion or 15% from year-end 2018. The company's leverage ratio of gross par to statutory capital declined to 21 to one, down from 23 to one at year-end 2018. The company also discussed its corporate segment balance sheet and the insurance companies. The corporate segment had total assets of approximately $1 billion as of December 31, 2019, with unencumbered cash and liquid assets held by MBIA Inc. totaling $375 million as of year-end 2019.
