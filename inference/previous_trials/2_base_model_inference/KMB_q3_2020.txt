 

Kimberly-Clark Corporation reported third-quarter net sales of $4.7 billion, up 1% year-over-year, with organic sales increasing by 3%. The company's growth investments and market positions were improved, and it achieved cost savings and returned cash to shareholders. Adjusted gross margin was 36.2%, up 40 basis points year-over-year, and adjusted gross profit increased by 2%. The company's adjusted operating profit was down 6% and operating margin was 17.2%, down 130 basis points versus year ago. For the full year, the company expects net sales to grow 2% to 3%, and adjusted earnings per share of $7.50 to $7.65, representing year-on-year growth of 9% to 11%. The company's recent acquisition of Softex is expected to improve its underlying growth prospects and expand its presence in high-growth markets.
