 

Quaker Houghton reported its first quarter 2020 earnings and provided an update on its performance during the COVID-19 pandemic. Despite the impact of the pandemic, which reduced sales by about 4% and temporarily halted Boeing's 737 MAX production, the company's total organic volume growth due to net share gains was approximately 2% in the first quarter of this year versus the first quarter of last year. The combined gross margins of Quaker Houghton were expected to be about 1% lower than stand-alone Quaker, but in the first quarter comparison, the difference is only 0.5 percentage point. This is indicative of the raw material savings from the integration starting to come through. The company's pro forma adjusted EBITDA grew 10% versus last year due to cost savings, the impact of the Norman Hay acquisition, and additional cost control measures. The company took fast action to save costs in numerous ways, including eliminating discretionary expenses, stopping new hires, executive pay cuts, and furloughing some positions. The company also reviewed its integration's synergy plans in light of the pandemic and took additional actions to accelerate other synergies.
