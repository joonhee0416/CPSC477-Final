 2021 Earnings Call Summary

Hudson Pacific Properties reported strong financial performance in the third quarter of 2021, with robust same property NOI growth and solid leasing activity. The company's focus on top-tier tech and media markets, building and reinvesting in a state-of-the-art portfolio, and maintaining a strong balance sheet have been critical factors in its success. The third quarter saw a positive momentum around tenants' return to the office, with some companies already reiterating at least a portion of their employees. The company also made several major announcements around the successful growth of its studio platform, including the unveiling of plans for Sunset Glenoaks, a purpose-built studio in Los Angeles, and the purchase of Star Waggons and Zio Studio Services, enhancing its existing clients' experience and capturing significant additional production-related revenue. Hudson Pacific Properties received its GRESB 2021 real estate assessment results, showcasing its commitment to being a leader on ESG issues, earning GRESB Green Star, the highest 5-star ratings for the third year in a row, and being named an office sector leader for the America's ranking first among the 22 companies in that category. The company's rent collections remained strong, with 99% for its portfolio overall and 100% for office and studio tenants. The third quarter NOI at its 45 consolidated same-store office properties increased 5.1% on a GAAP basis and increased 10.8% on a cash basis, while for its three same-store studio properties, NOI increased 49.8% on a GAAP basis and 45.5% on a cash basis. The company has $0.6 billion in liquidity with no material maturities until 2023 and an average loan term of 4.6 years.
