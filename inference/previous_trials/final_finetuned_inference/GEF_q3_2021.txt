 2021 Third Quarter Earnings Call Summary

Greif delivered robust third-quarter results, with record quarterly adjusted EBITDA of $238 million and adjusted class A earnings per share of $1.93. The company's leverage ratio fell to 2.8 times, and its board approved a $0.02 and a $0.03 increase to its class A and class B quarterly dividend, respectively. Greif's global industrial packaging business delivered outstanding third-quarter results, with global steel drum volume increasing by 8% per day. The company's third-quarter net sales, excluding the impact of foreign exchange, rose 34% versus the prior-year quarter, due to stronger volumes and higher selling prices. Greif anticipates generating between $335 million and $365 million in adjusted free cash flow in fiscal 2021, with a bias to the upside of that range. The company expects to benefit from ongoing strength and improving trends in its key end markets, with its extensive global portfolio, differentiated service capability, and sharp focus on operational execution enabling it to best serve its customer needs while generating significant shareholder value.
