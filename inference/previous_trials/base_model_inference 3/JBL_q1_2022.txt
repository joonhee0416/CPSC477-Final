 1

Jabil delivered strong results in the first fiscal quarter of 2022, with $400 million of core operating income on $8.6 billion in revenue, resulting in a core operating margin of 4.7%. The team's success was driven by broad-based strength in several key end markets, including healthcare, industrial and semi cap, and mobility, as customary for the first fiscal quarter. Despite the chaotic global supply chain environment, the team was able to accomplish all of this.

# 2

Jabil's procurement and supply chain team noted that demand continues to outstrip supply, particularly as it relates to semiconductors, an issue that has persisted since 2019. However, Jabil continues to leverage best-in-class tools and relationships to maximize its allocation and keep the factories in production to the best of its ability. The team anticipates continued supply chain challenges, which have been incorporated in the guidance similar to previous quarters.

# 3

Jabil's first quarter segment results showed that revenue for the DMS segment was $4.7 billion, an increase of 11.1% on a year-over-year basis, with strength across the healthcare, automotive, and mobility businesses. The strong year-over-year performance in the EMS segment was also broad-based, with strength across the digital print and retail, industrial and semi cap, and 5G wireless and cloud businesses.

# 4

Jabil's cash flows and balance sheet showed that inventory days came in at 66 days, a decline of five days sequentially. The management team continues to be fully committed to delivering strong results for the company.
