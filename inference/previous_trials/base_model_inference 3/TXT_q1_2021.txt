 2021 First Quarter Earnings Call Summary

Textron reported strong earnings for the first quarter of 2021, with revenues of $2.9 billion and adjusted net income of $0.70 per share. Segment profit for the quarter was $256 million, up from $156 million in the first quarter of 2020. The manufacturing cash flow before pension contributions totaled $71 million, up $501 million from last year's first quarter.

At Bell, revenues were up in the quarter on higher commercial revenues, partially offset by lower military revenues. The company delivered 17 helicopters in the quarter and saw solid order activity in multiple end markets. Bell is currently building the Invictus 360 prototype for FLRAA and has demonstrated impressive performance milestones for the V-280 aircraft.

Textron Systems reported flat revenues for the quarter but executed well with improved operating margins. The business was awarded a contract to up to $607 million from the U.S. Army for the sustainment and modernization of existing shadow systems to the upgraded Block III configuration. Systems is also responding to the FTUAS RFI and delivered the first RCV medium prototype to the Army customer.

At ATAC, the company is ramping F1 flight hours at the operating sites related to the three awards of the U.S. Air Force CAPCAS program. At the end of the quarter, ATAC had 16 F1 aircraft certified for operation and deployed across customer sites.
