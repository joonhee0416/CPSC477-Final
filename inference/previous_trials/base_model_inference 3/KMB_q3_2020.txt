 

Kimberly-Clark Corporation (KCC) reported its third-quarter earnings results, with organic sales increasing by 3% compared to the same period last year. The company also increased its growth investments and achieved cost savings, returning cash to shareholders. Despite the COVID-19 pandemic, KCC's net sales were up 1% from the year ago, driven by increased demand and higher volumes. The company's adjusted gross margin was 36.2%, up 40 basis points year-on-year, and adjusted operating profit was down 6% compared to the same period last year. KCC expects organic sales growth of 5% for the full year, compared to its previous estimate of 4% to 5%, and a new outlook for adjusted earnings per share of $7.50 to $7.65, representing year-on-year growth of 9% to 11%. The company also announced the acquisition of Softex, a high-growth market in the diaper industry in Indonesia, which is projected to nearly triple in size over the next decade. KCC is optimistic about its opportunities to generate long-term growth and create shareholder value and is investing in its brands and improving its market positions.
