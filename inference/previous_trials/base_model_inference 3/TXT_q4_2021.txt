 2021 Earnings Summary: Textron

Textron reported revenues of $3.3 billion in the fourth quarter of 2021, down from $3.7 billion in the same quarter of 2020. The company recorded a pre-tax special charge of $5 million related to the 2020 restructuring plan. Excluding these special charges, adjusted income from continuing operations was $0.94 per share for the fourth quarter of 2021, compared to $1.06 per share in the same quarter of 2020. Segment profit in the quarter was $310 million, down $14 million from the same quarter of 2020.

The aviation segment reported strong performance, with aircraft utilization improving and a backlog of $2.5 billion at year-end. The segment delivered 167 jets and 125 commercial turboprops in 2021. Bell reported a solid year, with commercial helicopters delivered at a rate of 156 units and a backlog of $2.1 billion. Textron Systems reported a full-year margin of 14.8%, up 320 basis points from the previous year. The segment delivered the fourth Ship-to-Shore Connector to the U.S. Navy and received a $82 million logistics support contract for the Shadow program.

Looking ahead, Textron expects revenues of about $5.5 billion in 2022, with segment margins ranging from 10% to 11%. The company forecasts earnings per share in the range of $3.80 to $4 per share and manufacturing cash flow before pension contributions of about $700 million to $800 million. R&D
