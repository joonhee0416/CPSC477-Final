 2019 APS Earnings Call Summary

The transcript covers the 2019 achievements and provides updates on operating and regulatory matters. The company's strategy is anchored by four concepts: clean, affordable, reliable, and customer-focused. The clean concept involves decarbonizing the generation mix and delivering 100% clean carbon-free electricity by 2050. The affordable concept aims to maintain reasonable electricity prices while growing the customer base and increasing shareholder value. The reliable concept focuses on maintaining high-performing well-run resources and acquiring resources that balance reliability, cost, and flexibility. The customer-focused concept involves developing innovative programs and connecting customers with advanced technologies to manage their bills. The company plans to strengthen its relationships with stakeholders, including regulators and customers, and work collaboratively with those who have a vested interest in Arizona's future and the company's role as the state's largest electricity provider. The company expects to issue equity sometime after 2020, with an expected amount in the $300 million to $400 million range. The financial health of the company will continue to provide customers with low-cost access to capital and competitive returns to shareholders. The company anticipates an effective tax rate of 14% in 2020, with continued use of income tax incentives reducing cash taxes in the year projects are placed in service. The company's pension remains healthy, with a funded status of 97% as of year-end 2019. The company saw high-profile data centers and manufacturing plants break ground in the West Valley, with Arizona's population growing by approximately 120,000 people between July 2018 and July 2019. The Metro Phoenix area continues to show strong job growth, with employment increasing 2.9%
