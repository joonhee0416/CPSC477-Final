 

Graham Corporation reported strong financial results for the fiscal 2021 second quarter, with $28 million in sales, up from $21.6 million in the same quarter last year. The company's defense sales were $9.4 million, with a quick turn material order making up approximately half of the revenue in the Navy market. The quarter benefited from an acceleration of some work at an Asian subcontractor. The company's gross profit increased to $7.7 million, up from $4.9 million last year, primarily due to volume. Gross margins were 27.5%, up from 22.9%. EBITDA margins were 14.2%, up from 7.8% in the second quarter of last year. The company's net income was $2.7 million, up from $1.2 million last year. For the first half of fiscal 2021, sales were $44.7 million, up from $42.2 million in the first half of last year. The company's gross profit was $9.3 million, slightly off the $9.7 million last year, and gross margins were down 20.7% versus 22.9% last year. The company's year-to-date EBITDA margins were 4.9% versus 4.5% in the first half of last year, and net income was $900,000 or $0.09 per share, down from $1.3 million or $0.13 per share last year. The company's backlog improved to $114.9 million, with 60% of the backlog planned to convert during the next four quarters.
