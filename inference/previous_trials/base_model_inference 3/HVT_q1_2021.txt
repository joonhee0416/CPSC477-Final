 

Havertys reported strong results for the first quarter of 2021, with a 31.8% increase in delivered sales over the prior year quarter. The company's gross profit margin increased by 160 basis points, and income before income tax increased by $23.1 million. Havertys' balance sheet showed an increase in inventories and customer deposits, and the company ended the quarter with $210 million of cash and cash equivalents. The company expects its gross margins for 2021 to be between 56.5% and 57%, and anticipates fixed and discretionary SG&A expenses to be in the $265 million to $268 million range. Havertys plans to invest in new stores, remodels, expansions, and information technology in 2021.
