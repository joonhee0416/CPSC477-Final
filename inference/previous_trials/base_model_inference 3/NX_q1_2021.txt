 

Quanex Corporation reported strong financial results for the first quarter of 2021, with revenue of $230.1 million, an increase of 17.1% compared to the same period in 2020. The increase was primarily due to increased demand for products across all product lines and operating segments. Net income for the quarter was $7.9 million or $0.24 per diluted share, compared to $10,000 or $0.00 per diluted share in the same period in 2020. On an adjusted basis, net income increased to $9 million or $0.27 per diluted share. The company's EBITDA increased by 55.4% to $24.3 million, with an adjusted EBITDA margin expansion of approximately 260 basis points. The company's balance sheet is strong, with a liquidity position that is solid, and a leverage ratio of net debt to last 12 months adjusted EBITDA that is unchanged at 0.6 times. The company is raising its expectations for the year and now expects approximately 12% sales growth in its North American fenestration segment, approximately 5% sales growth in its North American cabinet components segment, and approximately 22% sales growth in its European fenestration segment. The company expects net sales to be up approximately 25% year over year in Q2, with the strongest revenue growth and margin expansion in Q2 likely coming from its European fenestration segment. The company is also facing challenges such as labor shortages and inflationary pressures.
