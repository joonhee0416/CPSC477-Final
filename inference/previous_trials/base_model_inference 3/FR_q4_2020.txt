 2020 Earnings Summary

The FR team delivered outstanding results for shareholders in 2020, despite the uncertain operating environment. The portfolio performance was strong, with high occupancy levels and cash rent growth. In the fourth quarter, the company delivered year-end occupancy of 95.7%, up 70 basis points from the guidance midpoint provided on the third quarter call. The positive fourth quarter leasing statistics nationally are consistent with the company's experience, as CBRE's preliminary figure for net absorption is 104 million square feet, the highest quarterly result in the last four years. For the full year, net absorption was 224 million square feet, 15% higher than 2019. The company expects to capitalize on its current land holdings and new acquisitions to generate more growth and value creation in 2021. The company is focused on making progress in realizing the three-year cash flow growth opportunity laid out at its Investor Day. The company has signed leases for approximately 54% of its 2021 rollovers and a cash rental rate increase of approximately 13%. The company's expirations for the balance of 2021 are fairly granular, with its largest remaining rollover now a 400,000 square footer where the tenant is expected to vacate in May. The company has signed a long-term lease for 100% of the three-building First Cypress Creek Commerce Center with a single e-commerce tenant. The company has also signed and commenced leases for both the 358,000 and the 72,000 square foot facilities in the fourth quarter at the First Redwood project in the Inland Empire.
