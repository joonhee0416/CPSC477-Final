 

The company's Q3 results highlight strong demand across all three technologies, with revenue growth of 13%, earnings per share growth of 21%, and operating margins expansion by 150 basis points. The company ended the quarter with a record Q3 backlog of $11.4 billion, up 7% from last year. In Products and Systems, demand for LMR and video security remained robust, while supply chain constraints impacted the PCR business. In Software and Services, strong demand drove revenue growth and improved profitability, with Command Center Software revenue growth expected to be low double digits and video security and access control software revenue growth likely to be greater than 35%. The company expects full year sales to be up 10% to 10.25%, full year earnings per share between $9 and $9.04 per share, and full year operating cash flow to be approximately $1.825 billion.
