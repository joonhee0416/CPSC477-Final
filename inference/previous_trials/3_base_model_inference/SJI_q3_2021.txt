 

SJI reported solid performance in the third quarter and through the first nine months of 2021, with economic earnings increasing by 12% or approximately $12 million. The company's utilities, South Jersey Gas and Elizabethtown Gas, represent the bulk of its earnings, with utility margin growth remaining strong. The company is also investing in infrastructure modernization and energy efficiency programs to reduce methane emissions. SJI is pursuing regulatory initiatives, including the construction of a liquefied natural gas facility and the request for $742 million in Phase 3 infrastructure modernization investments at South Jersey Gas. The company's nonutility operations, including its energy management and energy production segments, also delivered solid results. SJI's capital expenditures and clean energy investments for the year-to-date were approximately $434 million, with more than 80% of this amount allocated for regulated utility investments. The company has ample liquidity and no significant debt maturities due in the near term. SJI is reaffirming its expectation for 2021 economic earnings of $1.55 to $1.6
