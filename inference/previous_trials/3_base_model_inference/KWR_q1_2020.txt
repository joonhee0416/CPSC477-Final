 2020 Q1 Earnings Call Summary

Quaker Houghton reported its first quarter 2020 results, which were better than expected due to integration savings, cost control measures, and additional synergies. The company's first quarter pro forma adjusted EBITDA grew 10% versus last year, and its non-GAAP earnings per share were $1.38, down from $1.41 in Q1 last year. Despite the COVID-19 pandemic, the company's 34 plants around the world are operating, and it is meeting customers' needs. The company took share in the marketplace as it estimates total organic volume growth due to net share gains was approximately 2% in the first quarter of this year versus the first quarter of last year. The company's combined gross margins were expected to be about 1% lower than stand-alone Quaker, but in the first quarter comparison, the difference is only 0.5 percentage point.
