 2020 Earnings Summary

The company ended the year with a record of more than $1.5 billion of premiums in force. However, the industry and markets it served experienced a record-setting frequency of weather events during the 2020 winter season, which impacted the 4th quarter and full year results. The company had exposure to several named 2020 storms and PCS events, resulting in over 10,000 claims in the 4th quarter. Despite the weather, the company continued to focus on underwriting and increasing its primary rates in Florida and other states. It also continued to implement new binding guidelines to address emerging loss trends and maintain a resilient balance sheet. The company had secured almost 75% of its first event reinsurance capacity for June 1, 2021, and continued to develop adaptive adjusting approaches to address claims loss cost trends. The company looks forward to 2021, focusing on resiliency and taking necessary steps to provide reliability to consumers and reduce uncertainty for shareholders.
