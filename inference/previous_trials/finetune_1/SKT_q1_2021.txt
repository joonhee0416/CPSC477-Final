 

Tanger Outlets reported a 20 basis point increase in consolidated portfolio occupancy to 91.7% in the first quarter, despite ongoing pressure on retenanting spreads. Blended average rental rates decreased 2.8% on a straight-line basis and 8.5% on a cash basis, reflecting a 300 basis-point improvement on a cash basis and a 390 basis-point improvement on a straight-line basis compared to the reported Q4 2020 spreads. Domestic traffic returned to 97% of the 2019 level in the first quarter, despite February traffic being impacted by severe winter weather and operating at 20% fewer hours. The company collected 95% of contractual fixed rents billed in the first quarter of 2021 and has collected 96% of the deferred 2020 rents due to be repaid in the first quarter. The company expects store closures during 2021 related to bankruptcies and brandwide restructurings to total approximately 200,000 square feet, including the 61,000 square feet it recaptured during the first quarter. The company's guidance assumes there are no further domestic government-mandated shutdowns and assumed lease termination fees decrease by $9 million to $10 million, or $0.09 to $0.10 per share from the elevated level it recognized in 2020. The company expects core FFO per share for 2021 to be between $1.47 and $1.57.
