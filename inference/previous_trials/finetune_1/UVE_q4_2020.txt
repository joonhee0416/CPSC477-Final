 2020 Earnings Call Summary

The company ended the year with a record of more than $1.5 billion of premium in force. However, the industry and markets served experienced a record-setting frequency of weather events during the 2020 winter season, which impacted the company's 4th quarter and full year results. The company had exposure to several named storms and PCS weather events, resulting in over 10,000 claims in the fourth quarter. Despite the weather in 2020, the company continued to focus on underwriting, increasing its primary rates in Florida and reinsurance costs. The company also continued to implement new binding guidelines to address emerging loss trends, maintained a resilient balance sheet, and continued to be backed by its great reinsurance program. The company continued its geographic expansion efforts and implemented its catastrophe rapid response teams during the COVID-19 pandemic. The company also continued to develop adaptive adjusting approaches to address claims loss cost trends. The company ended 2020 with total revenue up 14.2%, driven primarily by growth in net premiums earned, realized gains on investments, and increases in service revenue. The company's direct premiums written grew by 21.9% in Q4 compared to the prior year's quarter, led by the impact of rate increases in Florida and other states taking effect as well as strong direct premium growth in Q4 of 18.9% in states outside of Florida. The combined ratio decreased 18.9 points for the quarter to 124%, but increased 9.7 points for the full year to 113.6%. The full year increases were driven primarily by an increase of 13 points for increased weather in 2020. The company expects a GAAP and non-GAAP adjusted earnings per share range
