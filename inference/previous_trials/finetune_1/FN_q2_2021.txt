 

The company had another record quarter with Q2 results that exceeded guidance and supported the company's longer-term optimism. The company's revenue in the second quarter was a record $453.8 million, representing year-over-year as well as sequential growth. The company's gross and operating margins were at their highest levels in over a year. The company's second quarter played out largely as anticipated, with an unexpected positive surprise from automotive programs, which represented the most significant driver of upside in the quarter. The company's automotive revenue grew more than 30% from the first quarter and more than doubled from a year ago. The company is optimistic that this momentum in the automotive space will continue into the third quarter. The company's industrial laser revenue was approximately flat and was slightly better than expected. The company expects industrial laser revenue to improve in the third quarter and is optimistic about the longer-term potential for increased outsourcing from this market. The company's optical communications revenue was approximately flat, but saw modest growth in the second quarter. The company is optimistic that datacom revenue will show an improvement in the third quarter and that both datacom and telecom revenue should increase sequentially. The company is advancing its plans to expand its manufacturing footprint and has broken ground on a new 1 million square foot building at its campus in Chonburi, Thailand. The company expects construction to take approximately 1.5 years, which means it could start to see revenue from this facility in approximately two years. The company is excited about the growth from new programs in multiple areas, coupled with its ability to expand margins. The company continues to aggressively pursue new opportunities that will further its position and drive growing revenue and profitability and enhance long-term shareholder value.
