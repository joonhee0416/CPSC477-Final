 2021 Earnings Call Transcript Summary

Havertys, a furniture retailer, reported a 31.8% increase in delivered sales in the first quarter of 2021 compared to the same period last year. The increase was driven by higher traffic, closing rates, and average ticket prices. The company's gross profit margin increased by 160 basis points in the first quarter of 2021 compared to the same period last year. Havertys expects to open a new store in Northeast Austin, Texas later this year and is actively investigating and pursuing new store opportunities. The company's supply chain team faced many headwinds during the first quarter, including availability of container capacity, container freight costs, port delays, and weather-related disruptions. Despite these challenges, Havertys was able to receive approximately 10% more product in Q1 versus Q4. The company expects to be able to match or exceed the same flow of product in Q2. Havertys expects its gross margins for 2021 to be between 56.5% and 57%, and its fixed and discretionary type SG&A expenses for 2021 to be in the $265 million to $268 million range. The company's anticipated effective tax rate in 2021 is expected to be 24%.
