 2020 Earnings Call Transcript Summary

NHI, a senior housing and skilled nursing operator, reported its first quarter earnings for the period ending March 31, 2020. The company's net income per diluted common share for the quarter was $1.37, compared to $0.83 in the same period in 2019. NHI's three FFO performance metrics for the first quarter were NAREIT FFO, normalized FFO, and adjusted FFO or AFFO, which increased by 3.1%, 3.8%, and 5.7%, respectively. The company's cash NOI, a metric used to measure performance, was $108.8 million for the quarter, down from $114.7 million in the same period in 2019.

The COVID-19 pandemic has had a significant impact on NHI's business, with operators implementing protocols to prevent or limit the spread of the virus resulting in a significant downturn in inquiries, tours, and move-ins. This has negatively impacted occupancy, particularly in senior living communities, which have seen average occupancy decline by 150 basis points from March to April. The skilled nursing portfolio has also been affected, with 17 of NHI's 78 SNFs having active resident cases as of the last update. However, the government has provided financial support to the SNF industry, and NHI expects to gain liquidity through various programs.

NHI's management team and Board have deep experience managing during times of crisis, and the company's balance sheet and liquidity, together with strong lender and market relationships, put it on firm footing.
