 1

The company's revenue for the first quarter of 2021 was $640 million higher than the same period last year, and operating earnings were up $4 million. The operating margin for the entire company was 10%, which was 70 basis points lower than the year-ago quarter. The company beat consensus by $0.18 per share, with revenue of $500 million more than anticipated by the sell side and almost $50 million more in operating earnings.

# 2

The aerospace segment had revenue of $1.9 billion and operating earnings of $220 million, with an 11.7% operating margin. Gulfstream alone had a book-to-bill of 1.34:1. The company experienced a high level of interest and activity, with Gulfstream enjoying a 5.7% increase in service revenue. The G500 and G600 continued to perform well, with margins improving on a consistent basis. The G700 is expected to enter service in the fourth quarter of 2022.

# 3

The combat systems segment had revenue of $1.8 billion, up 6.6% over the year-ago quarter. The company expects the second quarter to be its most challenging from a delivery perspective, but it also expects rapid improvement in the third and fourth quarters as it had planned for an increase in production and more delivery.

# 4

The company's cash performance in the quarter was markedly improved from previous years, with operating cash flow essentially breaking even and free cash flow of negative $131 million.
