 2021 Q1 Earnings Call Summary

Textron reported strong first quarter results, with revenues up 3.8% and adjusted net income of $0.70 per share. Segment margins increased by 330 basis points, driven by strong execution across all segments. Bell's commercial revenues were up 11.3%, with 17 helicopters delivered. FLRAA contract modification of $293 million was awarded for the second phase of the competitive demonstration and reduction program. Textron Systems received a contract to up to $607 million from the U.S. Army for the sustainment and modernization of existing shadow systems. Systems also delivered the first RCV medium prototype to the Army customer. At Aviation, revenues were flat, but backlog grew by $450 million. The Cessna SkyCourier aircraft certification program is progressing well, with a target entry into service in the second half of the year. Specialized vehicles revenue was up 15.3%, driven by higher volume and price. Kautex saw its volume of fuel systems for hybrid electric vehicles more than double to about 9% of total production volume. Textron raised its guidance for adjusted earnings per share to a range of $2.80 to $3.00 per share, with a manufacturing cash flow before pension contributions target of $600 million to $700 million.
