 2021 Earnings Call Summary

The earnings call for the year ended December 31, 2021, was held by Scott Lauber, President and CEO; Xia Liu, CFO; and Beth Straka, Senior Vice President of Corporate Communications and Investor Relations. The company exceeded its most recent guidance, with earnings per share coming in at $4.07, an increase of 6.4% from the previous year. The positive results were driven by favorable weather, a solid economic recovery in the region, and the company's continued focus on operating efficiency. The balance sheet and cash flows remain strong, allowing the company to fund a highly executable capital plan without issuing equity. The company's ESG progress plan, which is focused on efficiency, sustainability, and growth, is expected to support average growth in the asset base of 7% per year, driving earnings growth, dividend growth, and dramatically improved environmental performance. The company is also actively shaping the future of clean energy, with a goal of achieving net-zero carbon emissions from power generation by 2050. The company is investing in nearly 2,400 megawatts of renewable capacity over the next five years. The company is also participating in a hydrogen power pilot program, which aims to test hydrogen as a fuel source at one of its newer natural gas-powered units located in the Upper Peninsula of Michigan. The company is also driving forward on its $17.7 billion ESG progress plan, which is expected to support average growth in the asset base of 7% per year.
