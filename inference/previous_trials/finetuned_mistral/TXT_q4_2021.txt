"1 Earnings Call Summary

Textron reported its fourth quarter earnings for 2021, with revenues of $3.3 billion, down from $3.7 billion in the previous quarter. The company recorded a pre-tax special charge of $5 million related to its 2020 restructuring plan, resulting in an adjusted income from continuing operations of $0.94 per share. Segment profit in the quarter was $310 million, down $14 million from the previous year.

The aviation segment had a strong quarter, with improved aircraft utilization, low preowned inventory levels, and strong customer demand. Order activity remained high, resulting in a $4.1 billion backlog at year-end. The segment delivered 167 jets and 125 commercial turboprops in 2021, up from the previous year.

The defense segment also had a solid year, with a segment margin of 10.1% in the fourth quarter. The segment delivered 156 helicopters in 2021, up from the previous year.
