 2021 Q1 Earnings Call Summary

Textron reported strong financial performance in Q1 2021, with revenues of $2.9 billion and adjusted net income of $0.70 per share. Segment margins were up 330 basis points, driven by strong execution across all segments. Bell reported higher commercial revenues, offset by lower military revenues, and delivered 17 helicopters in the quarter. The company is continuing with its build of the Invictus 360 prototype for FLRAA and has delivered the first RCV medium prototype to the Army customer. Systems received a contract to up to $607 million from the U.S. Army for the sustainment and modernization of existing shadow systems to the upgraded Block III configuration. Aviation delivered 28 jets and 14 commercial turboprops in the quarter, with order activity resulting in backlog growth of $450 million. The company is also working on the certification program for the Cessna SkyCourier aircraft. Revenues at Specialized Vehicles were up from last year's first quarter, primarily driven by higher volume and price in the specialized vehicle product line. Kautex saw its volume of fuel systems for hybrid electric vehicles more than double in the quarter. Textron raised its expected guidance of adjusted earnings per share to a range of $2.80 to $3 per share, and reiterated its outlook for manufacturing cash flow before pension contributions of $600 million to $700 million with planned pension contributions of $50 million.
