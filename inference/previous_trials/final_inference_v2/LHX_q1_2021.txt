 1

Harris reported strong first quarter results, with organic revenue up approximately 2% and mid-single-digit growth in core government businesses, partially offset by COVID-related impacts of about three points from commercial businesses. This was the last quarter of tough compares due to the pandemic, and the team managed the impacts incredibly well. The company's margins expanded by 140 basis points to 18.9%, resulting in earnings per share of $3.18, up 14% and ahead of internal targets. Free cash flow was $630 million supported by shareholder returns in excess of $900 million, including repurchases of $700 million from the recently authorized $6 billion program, with a balance from dividends following the 20% increase in March.

# 2

The company's CEO, who is stepping down as planned on June 29, expressed his pride in the people at L3Harris who work hard every day to support customers' critical missions and deliver value to shareholders. The performance culture and work environment the company created is special, and they were recently recognized for it by Fortune as a 100 best company to work for in 2021 and earlier this year as a world's most admired company. The CEO also mentioned that the independent directors of the Board unanimously endorsed the transition occurring as planned in the merger agreement, indicating their confidence and mind in the incoming CEO's ability to lead the company going forward.
