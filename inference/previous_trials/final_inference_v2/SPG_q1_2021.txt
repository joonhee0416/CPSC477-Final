Taubman Centers reported a significant improvement in its profitability and cash flow for the first quarter of 2021, with funds from operations of $934 million or $2.48 per share. The company's international operations were negatively impacted by governmental closure orders and capacity restrictions, resulting in a decline in first-quarter FFO of approximately $0.08 per share compared to expectations.

# 2.

Taubman Centers also recorded additional COVID impacts in the first quarter of approximately $0.07 per share from domestic rent abatements and uncollectible rents. The company generated $875 million in cash from operations in the quarter, which was an increase of 18% compared to the prior-year period.

# 3.

Taubman Centers collected over 95% of its net billed rents for the first quarter and its in-line tenant collections are back to pre-COVID levels in the approximate 98% range. The company's operating metrics in the period were as follows: mall and outlet occupancy at the end of the first quarter was 90.8%, down 50 basis points compared to the fourth quarter of 2020.

# 4.

Leasing momentum has continued across Taubman Centers' portfolio, with the company signing 1,100 leases for approximately 4.4 million square feet and having significant leases in its pipeline. The improving domestic economic environment, shopper sentiment, and increased shopper foot traffic and sales across the portfolio have been encouraging.
