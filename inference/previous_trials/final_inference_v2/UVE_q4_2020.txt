 2020 Earnings Summary

The company ended the year with a record of over $1.5 billion in premiums in force. However, the industry and markets served experienced a record-setting frequency of weather events during the 2020 winter season, which negatively impacted the 4th quarter and full year results. The company had exposure to several named 2020 storms and other PCS events, resulting in over 10,000 claims in the fourth quarter. Despite the weather, the company continued to focus on underwriting, increasing its primary rates in Florida and reinsurance costs in some other states. The company also continued to implement new binding guidelines to address emerging loss trends, maintained a resilient balance sheet, and was backed by its reinsurance program. The company continued its geographic expansion efforts and implemented catastrophe rapid response teams during the COVID-19 pandemic, which accelerated the use of digital technology for adjusting claims. The company generated a loss of $0.57 on a GAAP basis and a loss of $0.84 on a non-GAAP adjusted basis for the quarter. For the full year, the company generated a loss of $0.90 on a non-GAAP adjusted basis. The direct premiums written grew by 21.9% in Q4 compared to the prior year's quarter, driven by rate increases in Florida and other states, as well as strong direct premium growth in Q4 of 18.9% in states outside of Florida. The combined ratio decreased 18.9 points for the quarter but increased 9.7 points for the full year, driven primarily by an increase of 13 points for increased weather in 2020. Unrealized gains were driven by market fluctuations in equity securities, resulting in a favorable outcome for the quarter and full year
