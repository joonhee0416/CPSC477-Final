Harris reported strong first quarter results, with organic revenue up approximately 2% and mid-single-digit growth in core government businesses, partially offset by COVID-related impacts in commercial businesses. This should be the last quarter of tough compares due to the pandemic.

Margins expanded by 140 basis points to 18.9%, resulting in earnings per share of $3.18, up 14% and ahead of internal targets. Free cash flow was $630 million, supported by shareholder returns in excess of $900 million.

The transition from CEO to Chris as successor is planned on June 29, with the independent directors of the Board unanimously endorsing the transition occurring as planned in the merger agreement.

The results speak to the momentum the company has in the future, and the strong foundation built for growth.

The strategic priorities identified for the next phase of the company under Chris' leadership are the foundations on which L3Harris will deliver sustainable top-line growth, steady margin expansion, and robust free cash flow with industry-leading capital returns.

The first quarter results, coupled with the Biden administration's announcement that the defense budget will continue to grow in FY '22, about 1.5 points versus FY '21, reinforces the optimism for growth.
