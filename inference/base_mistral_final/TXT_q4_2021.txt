 2021 Earnings Summary

Textron reported revenues of $3.3 billion in the fourth quarter, down from $3.7 billion in the same period last year. The company's adjusted income from continuing operations was $0.94 per share for the fourth quarter of 2021, up from $0.93 per share in the fourth quarter of 2020. Textron's manufacturing cash flow before pension contributions totaled $298 million in the quarter.

For the full year, revenues were $12.4 billion, up $731 million from last year. Adjusted income from continuing operations was $3.30 per share compared to $2.07 per share in 2020. The company's manufacturing cash flow before pension contributions was $1.1 billion, up from $596 million in 2020.

In the aviation segment, revenues were down slightly in the quarter, largely due to lower military revenues. However, the segment achieved a segment margin of 10.1% in the fourth quarter. Textron delivered 167 jets and 125 commercial turboprops in 2021.

In the defense segment, revenues were also down in the quarter, reflecting the continued wind down of the H-1 production
