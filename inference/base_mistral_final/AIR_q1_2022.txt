 2022 Q1 Earnings Call Summary

AAR reported strong results for the first quarter of fiscal year 2022, with sales up 14% and adjusted diluted earnings per share up 206% compared to the same period last year. The company's commercial sales increased 52%, while government and defense sales decreased 17%. The strong performance was driven by robust demand for airframe MRO services, with a significant majority of MRO volume coming from standard maintenance work. The company's operating margin was 5.5% for the quarter, up from 2.5% last year and 5.2% in the fourth quarter. AAR generated $18 million from operating activities and reduced the usage of its accounts receivable financing program. The company also announced several new business wins, including an exclusive agreement with Arkwin to distribute engine actuation and other commercial aviation products, a contract with the Department of Energy for the conversion and delivery of a 737-700 aircraft, and an extension of its component support program with Volotea. AAR's gross profit margin for the quarter was 14.2%, up from 12.1% in the prior year quarter, and adjusted gross profit margin was 16.1%.
