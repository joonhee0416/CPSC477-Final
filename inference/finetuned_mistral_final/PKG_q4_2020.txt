 

Packaging Corporation of America reported fourth quarter 2020 net income of $124 million or $1.30 per share, excluding special items. The company's EBITDA for the fourth quarter, excluding special items, was $293 million. The full year 2020 earnings excluding special items were $550 million or $5.78 per share. The company's Packaging segment EBITDA excluding special items was $1.2 billion with sales of $5.9 billion for the full year 2020. The company's Packaging segment demand remained very strong in 2020, with sales volumes in both the containerboard mills and the corrugated products plants setting all-time records. The company began producing high-performance virgin linerboard on the number three machine at Jackson, Alabama in the fourth quarter to help meet the continued strong demand from its box plant customers. The company expects to realize the majority of its recently announced Packaging segment price increases during the first quarter of 2021. However, the company also expects higher freight costs and labor costs in 2021.
