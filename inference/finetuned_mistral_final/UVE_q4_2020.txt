 2020 Earnings Summary

The company ended the year with a record of more than $1.5 billion of premium in force. However, the industry and the markets served experienced a record-setting frequency of weather events during the 2020 winter season, which impacted the fourth quarter and full-year results. The company was affected by several named storms and other PCS events, resulting in over 10,000 claims in the fourth quarter. Despite the weather in 2020, the company continued its focus on underwriting and increased its primary rates in Florida and some other states. The company also continued to implement new binding guidelines to address emerging loss trends and maintained a resilient balance sheet. The company has continued its geographic expansion efforts and implemented catastrophe rapid response teams during the COVID-19 pandemic, which accelerated the use of digital technology for adjusting claims. The company looks forward to 2021 as it continues to focus on resiliency and taking necessary steps to provide reliability to consumers and reduce uncertainty for shareholders.
