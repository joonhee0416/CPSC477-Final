 

In the first quarter of fiscal year 2022, AAR reported solid results with sales up 14% and adjusted diluted earnings per share up 206% compared to the same period last year. The commercial activities of the company showed a 17% growth sequentially, driven by the parts businesses and robust demand for airframe MRO services. The operating margin was 5.5% for the quarter, higher than the same quarter two years ago, despite a 16% decrease in revenue. AAR also announced several new business wins, including an exclusive agreement with Arkwin, a TransDigm company, and a contract with the Department of Energy. The company also extended its component support program with Volotea. AAR's WASS team played a critical role in the U.S. withdrawal from Afghanistan, transporting approximately 2,000 U.S. embassy personnel to Kabul International Airport. The company expects demand for its MRO activities to remain strong as airlines focus on readiness to support the recovery in air travel and preserving a healthy maintenance supply chain. The company also expects stable performance in the near term for part supply, while the exit from Afghanistan and programs nearing natural completion points will have a near-term impact on its government business.
