 

SJI, the parent company of South Jersey Gas and Elizabethtown Gas, delivered solid performance in the third quarter and through the first nine months of 2021, with economic earnings increasing by 12% or approximately $12 million. The utilities, South Jersey Gas and Elizabethtown Gas, represent the bulk of the company's earnings, with strong performance in utility margin growth, customer growth, positive rate case outcomes, infrastructure modernization programs, and effective O&M management. The company has also made significant investments in natural gas infrastructure, with over 12,000 new customers added in the last 12 months. SJI's regulatory initiatives continue to advance, with the BPU approving South Jersey Gas' engineering and route proposal for a planned 2 Bcf liquefied natural gas facility and requesting $742 million in Phase 3 infrastructure modernization investments. The company is also requesting $742 million in Phase 3 infrastructure modernization investments at South Jersey Gas. The company is reaffirming its expectation for 2021 economic earnings of $1.55 to $1.65 per diluted share, with significant step-ups expected in 2023 and 2025.
