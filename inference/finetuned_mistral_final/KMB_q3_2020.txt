 

The third quarter of 2020 saw an increase in organic sales by 3% for Kimberly-Clark Corporation, driven by the COVID-19 pandemic. The company also made significant growth investments and improved market positions. The cost savings were achieved through the FORCE program and restructuring initiatives, resulting in a combined savings of $140 million. The adjusted gross margin increased by 40 basis points year-on-year, and the adjusted operating profit was down 6% with an operating margin of 17.2%. The adjusted earnings per share were $1.72 in the quarter, compared to $1.84 in the year-ago period. The full-year outlook was raised, with organic sales growth of 5% expected, compared to the previous estimate of 4% to 5%. The company expects net sales to grow 2% to 3% this year, with a new outlook for adjusted earnings per share of $7.50 to $7.65. The acquisition of Softex Indonesia is expected to improve the underlying growth prospects in the personal care market in D&E markets.
