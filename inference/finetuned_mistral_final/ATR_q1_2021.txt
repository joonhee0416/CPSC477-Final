 2021 Aptar Earnings Call Summary

Aptar, a leading global provider of dispensing systems, announced its financial results for the first quarter of 2021. The company's revenue grew by 3%, driven by strong demand in the food and beverage segment, which increased by 14%. In the pharma segment, the company's adjusted EBITDA margin was comparable to the prior year, despite a mix shift and increased resin costs. The company's food and beverage segment delivered a stellar performance, driven by strong demand for innovative food dispensing closures. The beverage market, however, sales grew modestly due to the pass-through of higher resin prices. In the pharma segment, increased demand for injectable components, including those for COVID-19 vaccine distributions, and active material science solutions offset declines in the prescription drug and consumer healthcare markets. The company's balance sheet remained strong, with a leverage ratio of 1.4 and a gross capital of approximately 37%. The company expects a second quarter adjusted earnings per share in the range of $0.91 to $0.99 per share.
