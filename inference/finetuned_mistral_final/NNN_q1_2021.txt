 2021 Earnings Call Summary

The company announced an increase in its 2021 core FFO guidance from a range of $2.55 to $2.62 per share to a range of $2.70 to $2.75 per share. The increase was due to better-than-expected rent collections and the results from the first quarter.

The company's portfolio of 3,161 freestanding single-tenant retail properties continued to perform well, with occupancy at 98.3% at the end of the quarter, which remains above the long-term average. The company collected 97% of rents due for the first quarter and 98% of rents due for the month of April.

The company recently resolved two of its largest bankruptcies in favorable fashion. Chuck E. Cheese's affirmed all 53 of its leases, in exchange for a 25% temporary base rent reduction, and Ruby Tuesday's affirmed 26 of its 34 leases, accounting for over 80% of its annual rent.
