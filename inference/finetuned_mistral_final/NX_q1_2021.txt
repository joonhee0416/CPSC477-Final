 

Quanex Corporation reported strong financial results for the first quarter of 2021, with revenue increasing by 17.1% compared to the same period in 2020. The increase was driven by increased demand for products across all product lines and operating segments. The company reported net income of $7.9 million or $0.24 per diluted share, compared to $10,000 or $0.00 per diluted share during the three months ended January 31, 2020. On an adjusted basis, net income increased to $9 million or $0.27 per diluted share. The adjusted EBITDA for the quarter increased by 55.4% to $24.3 million, with an adjusted EBITDA margin expansion of approximately 260 basis points. The company's balance sheet remains strong, with a liquidity position of solid and a leverage ratio of net debt to last 12 months adjusted EBITDA of 0.6 times. The company is raising its expectations for the year and now expects approximately 12% sales growth in its North American fenestration segment, approximately 5% sales growth in its North American cabinet components segment and approximately 22% sales growth in its European fenestration segment. The company expects net sales to be up approximately 25% year over year in Q2
