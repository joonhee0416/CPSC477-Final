 

In the third quarter, Hudson Pacific experienced strong same-property NOI growth and solid leasing activity, with office and studio tenants continuing to pay rent and repay deferred rent. The company successfully executed on multiple city-related acquisitions, despite pandemic headwinds. The office market is showing positive indicators, with a shift away from short-term renewals to new and expansion deals. The company's studio platform is growing, with plans to build a large-scale purpose-built facility in the U.K and expand its services platform. The company's combined expertise with Blackstone is second to none, and it is poised to deliver and operate purpose-built next-generation studios. The company received strong GRESB 2021 real estate assessment results, earning GRESB Green Star and being named an office sector leader. The rent collections remained strong, with 99% for the portfolio overall and 100% for office and studio tenants. The third quarter NOI at the company's 45 consolidated same-store office properties increased 5.1% on a GAAP basis and 10.8% on a cash basis. The company has drawn $300 million under its revolving credit facility, leaving $300 million of undrawn capacity. The company is providing fourth-quarter 2021 guidance in the range of $1.95
