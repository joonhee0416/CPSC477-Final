 

Tanger Outlets reported a sustained rebound in traffic in the first quarter of 2021, returning to 97% of the 2019 level. The company's focus on rebuilding occupancy, driving leasing, and curating merchandise mix paid off, with blended average rental rates decreasing only 2.8% on a straight-line basis and 8.5% on a cash basis for all renewals and retenanted leases that commenced during the trailing 12 months ended March 31, 2021. The company also collected 95% of contractual fixed rents billed in the first quarter of 2021, with a run rate that suggests future collections will be strong. Tanger Outlets continues to prioritize maintaining a strong financial position and will focus on investing in its portfolio, reducing leverage, and evaluating selective growth opportunities over the longer term. The company expects store closures related to bankruptcies and brandwide restructurings to total approximately 200,000 square feet during 2021, with most of the recapture occurring during the first half of the year.
