The company reported strong Q3 results, with revenue growth of 13%, earnings per share growth of 21%, and operating margins expansion of 150 basis points. The company's backlog also reached a record $11.4 billion, up 7% from last year. The company's three technologies, LMR, video security and access control, and command center software, all experienced growth during the quarter. The company is raising its full-year guidance for both sales and EPS based on its strong Q3 results and expectations for the remainder of the year. The company's capital allocation in Q3 included $137 million in share repurchases, $120 million in cash dividends, and $61 million of capex. The company also closed the acquisition of Openpath, invested $50 million in equity securities of Evolv, and subsequently acquired Envysion. The company's Products and Systems segment saw strong growth in EMEA and Latin America, while the Software and Services segment saw strong growth in services and software contracts in North America.
