 2021 Earnings Call Summary

Textron reported its fourth quarter and full year 2021 financial results, with revenues of $3.3 billion and $12.4 billion, respectively. The company's aviation segment saw strong demand, with an improved operating environment and a segment margin of 10.1% in the fourth quarter. Bell's defense segment was awarded a $143 million contract for eight AT-6 aircraft, and Textron Systems delivered the fourth Ship-to-Shore Connector to the U.S. Navy. The company's industrial segment experienced lower volume and mix, with a segment profit of $38 million.

Looking ahead, Textron expects revenues of about $5.5 billion in 2022, with segment margins ranging from 10% to 11%. Bell forecasts revenues of about $3 billion, with a margin in the range of 10% to 11%. Textron Systems expects revenues of about $1.3 billion, with a margin in the range of 13.5% to 14.5%. The company expects a consolidated earnings per share range of $3.80 to $4 per share, with manufacturing cash flow before pension contributions projected to be about $700 million to $800 million. R&D is expected to be about $585 million, and capex is projected to be about $425 million in 2022.
