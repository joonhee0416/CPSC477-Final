 2021 Q3 Earnings Call Summary

Linde delivered another solid quarter, with Q2's record earnings per share performance continuing despite increasingly challenging market conditions. The company's operating cash flow and return on capital reached record levels, and its project backlog nearly doubled. Linde has been committed to reducing its greenhouse gas emissions footprint since the merger and has been delivering on this commitment. The operating culture at Linde runs deep, with employees taking pride in demonstrating continuous improvement across key operating metrics. The company's CEO, Steve, will become the Chairman of the Board, while Sanjiv will become CEO effective March first. Sanjiv has been appointed as CEO through a diligent three-year succession planning process, and Steve will continue to provide guidance as a Director and significant shareholder. The company's growth initiatives include secured project backlog, accretive base capex investments, and the strength of its growth in low capital intensity areas such as healthcare, food and beverage, engineering, and its packaged gas business. The company's project backlog has increased from $7.5 billion to $13.4 billion, and underlying sales grew 11% over prior year and 1% sequentially. The operating margin of 23.6% is 150 basis points above 2020, and the EPS of $2.73 is up 27% over last year. The company has a strong productivity culture, which enables consistent profit growth, and has a 16.7% return on capital, representing another record. The company has generated significant surplus cash and has access to very attractive capital through the debt markets. The fourth quarter earnings per share guidance range of $2.60 to $2.70 is 13% to 17% above last year and 38% to 43% above 2019. The company remains confident in its ability to continue delivering industry-leading performance, regardless of the macroeconomic conditions.
